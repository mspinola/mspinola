(11/29/2005) — The Carter's Commandos rolled over the Dunbar Dogs 102-52 in a big week 12 win. After the game some of the Carter's Commandos got into a brawl with the home crowd; suspensions and fines seem likely.

Carson Palmer had another outstanding game this week. Palmer threw for over 300 yards and found the end zone 3 times on perfect passes. Palmer has a league leading 24 touchdowns. Mike Vanderjagt had his most productive kicking game of the season with 4 field goals and 2 PATs. Vanderjagt is now 15 of 16 on the season in field goal attempts. Joey Galloway didn't score, but he still had a big game with 7 catches for 138 yards. Galloway has had four games with over 130 yards receiving.

"That's what I'm here to do," said Carter's Commandos ailing coach Chris Arenth of the team's solid play this week. "I'm here to be on the sidelines at crunch time, making the important decisions, calling plays. That's my job. Sometimes you're not 100 percent. Sometimes you're not 90. But you've got to go out there and give it your all."

Jamal Lewis finally showed some motivation this week as he plowed through defenders for 113 yards and his first touchdown in 5 weeks.

"We got after Carson Palmer," Dunbar Dogs coach Erik Farmerie said. "He gave us opportunities to blitz. We took advantage. But when it counted in the fourth quarter, we didn't get off the field."

Pittsburgh (11/29/2005) — LaDainian Tomlinson dominated with 45 points, helping the Manic Moose outpace the Blitzkrieg 79-55.

LaDainian Tomlinson had a huge second half with a 32-yard touchdown late in the fourth quarter and a 41-yarder in overtime. Tomlinson finished with over 200 yards of total offense. After back-to-back weeks of being shutout Brad Johnson found his groove this week with 3 touchdown passes to Marcus Robinson. Johnson finished with 207 yards and 1 interception.

It's funny when a running back disappoints owners by rushing for over 100 yards and scores a touchdown. (11/29/2005) — After hours of film study, the Karpet Munchers crafted a plan that handled everything the Iron City Pounders could throw at them, winning 125-94 and improving their record to 11-1.

Alge Crumpler had a huge game this week with 2 touchdowns and 104 yards receiving. Crumpler is now third among tight ends in receiving yards and touchdowns. Marvin Harrison started the game in style with an 80-yard touchdown reception and picked up 48 more yards during the game for his third 100-yard performance in his last four games. Brian Westbrook carried the ball a season high 21 times for his first 100-yard rushing game of the season. Westbrook also scored his longest touchdown of the season on a 27-yard run.

"It's all about us," said Karpet Munchers coach John Keenan. "If we come out and play our game, they can't play with us. Even if we give them some easy points, they still can't play with us."

Kyle Boller got off to a slow start, but he really picked up the pace in the second half. Boller finished with a season best 3 touchdown passes and his first 200 yard game. Jeremy Shockey caught a season high 10 balls for 127 yards and a touchdown that shouldn't have been. Shockey is tied for the touchdown lead and is moving up quickly in yardage. Santana Moss ended his 4-week scoring drought with a 22-yard touchdown catch. Moss totaled 65 yards on 6 catches this week.

"Playoffs? Playoffs! Are you kidding me? Playoffs?" Iron City Pounders coach Brian McIntyre said. "Shhhh! I'm trying to figure out how we are going to win another game."
