(12/13/2005) — For anyone who was wondering, Marvin Harrison is doing just fine.

Marvin Harrison opened up a 2-touchdown lead among receivers after scoring from 9 and 65 yards this week. Harrison caught 6 balls for a season high 137 yards. T.J. Houshmandzadeh is quickly becoming one of the most productive number 2 receivers in the league. Housh scored for the third week in a row and finished with 53 yards. Steve Smith maintained his narrow lead in receptions and yards with 5 catches for 103 yards. Smith has really cooled down in the scoring department with just 1 touchdown in his last 5 games.

Regarding the big game by Marvin Harrison, Karpet Munchers coach John Keenan said, "That's a plus. No question about that. We just asked him to take pride in himself and prove to me he is a football player."

Peyton Manning was impressive again this week with over 300 yards passing and 2 touchdowns. Manning is now the highest rated passer in the league and is tied for the lead in passing touchdowns. Warrick Dunn racked up over 100 yards of total offense, but he was kept out of the end zone for the fourth game in a row. Dunn now has over 1200 yards rushing on the season. Mike Anderson must frustrate owners to no end. Anderson followed up last week's outstanding performance with 8 carries for 21 yards and no touchdowns.

"The bottom line is, in the first half we gave up a silly touchdown pass, which happens in the third grade, fourth grade, or even in kindergarten," Otters coach Rich Raida said. "But that shouldn't have happened."

Pittsburgh (12/13/2005) — Matt Spinola acknowledged being nervous before the game, partly because of the outlandish “taking candy from a baby” comment on how the Manic Moose would have their way against the Blitzkrieg defense.

Koren Robinson didn't come close to having a repeat performance from last week, but he still has a nose for the end zone. Robinson scored on a 13-yard run. Antonio Gates turned a season high 13 catches into 123 yards and scored on an 8-yard catch. Gates is running away with the tight end lead in all major receiving categories. Eddie Kennison scored his longest touchdown of the season on a 47-yard reception and finished with 4 catches for 92 yards.

"There's no question you try to minimize the situation when one of your guys go out," said Manic Moose coach Matt Spinola. "But Koren Robinson brings us to a dimension we don't have unless he's in the ball game."

Drew Brees connected on two 8-yard touchdown passes and rushed for another as he completed a season high 35 of 52 passes for 279 yards. There's no telling how Drees will perform against Indy next week. Shaun Alexander maintained his league lead in rushing yards and touchdowns with 108 yards on the ground and a 3-yard touchdown run.

"I hate to lose anywhere," Blitzkrieg coach Sean Berecek said. "Losing to the Manic Moose is definitely tough to swallow."

(12/13/2005) — RB Domanick Davis scored 30 points to help the OakalDoakals extend their win streak, topping the Carter's Commandos 92-44.

Domanick Davis was a yardage machine for the second week in a row as he racked up 139 yards on 22 carries and 50 yards on 7 catches. David also scored on a short run. Samkon Gado had the best game of his young career with 171 yards rushing, including a 64-yard touchdown run. Gado is averaging over 100 yards rushing per game. Anquan Boldin delivered his fourth 100-yard game in a row as he pulled in 9 balls for 114 yards. Although Boldin missed 2 games, he has nearly caught Larry Fitzgerald in receptions and yards.

"I finally got the play I see all those other teams get every week," OakalDoakals coach Alan Hatok said. "Once I got that play, I said, 'Dang, something finally went your way."'

Corey Dillon may be getting healthy again, just in time for the fantasy playoffs. Dillon delivered his second 100-yard game of the season and scored on a 12-yard run. Mike Vanderjagt made all 4 of his field goal attempts for the second time in the last 3 games. Carson Palmer was kept in check this week as he completed a season low 13 of 27 passes for 93 yards and a short touchdown. Palmer still leads the league with 34 touchdowns.

"No more excuses, we need results. We've had too many excuses this season. No member of this team has any job security, including the coaches," Carter's Commandos coach Chris Arenth said.

(12/13/2005) — The game was a nightmare by halftime for the Ho Train -- and then the horror show got even worse for Jon Kraus.

Larry Johnson was amazing again this week with 143 yards rushing and 3 touchdowns. Although unlikely, Johnson could compete for the league's rushing title if he keeps up this pace. Tom Brady didn't let the bad weather affect his game this week as he completed 29 of 38 passes for 329 yards and 2 touchdowns. Brady also scored his first rushing touchdown of the season on a 4-yard run. Cadillac Williams had his most productive game of the season with 2 touchdowns and over 100 yards on the ground.

"I've got no reservations about our team," Texas Nashvillians coach Randy Folks said. "We've got football players. I felt like that all along, or I wouldn't have drafted them. They are all gamers."

Michael Vick delivered in a big way on Monday night with 2 rushing touchdowns and a trick play that resulted in a 54-yard touchdown pass. Vick finished with 231 yards in the air. Edgerrin James kept his 100-yard game streak alive with 93 yards on the ground and 9 catches for 61 yards. James has over 100 yards of total offense in every game this season. Steven Jackson is in the midst of a fantasy slump and there's no telling if he'll be able to break out of it. Jackson, who rushed for 67 yards, has scored just 1 touchdown in his last 5 games.

"This was a pretty good beating," Ho Train coach Jon Kraus said. "The score wasn't as bad really as I thought it was. Had we not held them a few times on defense, this could have gotten out of control."
