Pittsburgh (10/4/2005) — Matt Spinola had the last laugh after all. After many of the league’s coaches commented that the Manic Moose coach made a few lineup mistakes, enough points were put on the board to beat the Da Funk by a 102-53 margin.

There is no doubt that LT is in the groove. LaDainian Tomlinson followed up last week's explosion with 134 yards on the ground, 34 yards receiving, and 2 more rushing touchdowns. As hard as it is to believe, Terrell Owens keeps getting better each week. This week TO caught 11 passes for 171 yards and 1 touchdown. TO leads the league in receptions and yards. Eddie Kennison is finally getting the looks he deserves. Kennison turned in his second 100-yard game in a row and scored his first touchdown of the season.

"I know there was a turning point, but I didn't mark it down on my calendar," Manic Moose coach Matt Spinola said. "I just kept my head down, grinded through things and pushed and demanded because I felt this group is capable if they'll make the commitment."

Willis McGahee racked up over 100 yards of offense for the third time this season and scored on a 1-yard run. McGahee is averaging a very respectable 4.8 yards per carry, but unfortunately must deal with a weak passing attack. Byron Leftwich didn't have a great outing this week, but saved some face with a 45-yard touchdown strike. Leftwich was intercepted twice and also lost a fumble.

"You're not always going to get the breaks," Da Funk coach Mark Stahlsmith said. "Sure, you want the ball to bounce your way. But I've always said that the good teams overcome it."

(10/4/2005) — After spending four quarters trying to give away a victory, the F-Jackie got one anyway -- courtesy of the Blitzkrieg.

Donovan McNabb played through his pain and threw for his third 300-yard game in a row. McNabb also tossed 3 touchdowns, giving him a league leading 12 touchdowns this season. Reggie Wayne is still looking for his first big game of the season, but he gave owners some hope with his first touchdown. Wayne finished with 2 catches for 48 yards.

"Looking back," said coach Buddy Verdream, "my locker room tirade may have had a positive effect on the team when I started screaming and yelling, guys got behind me. Not that they weren't before, but this was an obvious slap in the face and guys weren't going to put up with that."

Drew Brees had another outstanding game this week, completing 79% of his passes for 248 yards and 2 touchdowns. Brees wasn't sacked and had no turnovers for the second week in a row. Donald Driver caught 6 passes for 92 yards and a score under the Monday night lights. Driver hasn't been able to deliver strong back-to-back performances yet this season. Donte' Stallworth was a ball hog this week, catching 8 balls for 129 yards. Stallworth caught more passes than the rest of the team combined, and tripled the rest of the team's receiving yardage.

"This is new for a lot of us, trying to win the close ones," Blitzkrieg coach Sean Berecek said. "We're in a bad habit right now, and something bad's happening to us near the end of the game. We're not making anything good happen to overcome it."

Plaxico Burress went crazy this week with 10 catches for 204 yards and 2 touchdowns. If Eli Manning can stay on track Plaxico could have a huge year. Joey Galloway poured it on this week with an 80-yard touchdown and 166 yards receiving. Galloway will have a great season if he continues to make the big plays. Palmer finished with 276 yards and now has an impressive 112 passer rating this season.

Keenan McCardell had a quiet week with just 1 catch for 11 yards. Lucky for owners, his sole reception turned into a touchdown. Brandon Lloyd followed up last week's impressive performance with 7 more catches for 102 yards. Although Lloyd didn't score this week, he is becoming a fantasy favorite at wide receiver.

"We needed to execute. We had some plays we didn't get done ... and they did a good job of taking things away," said OakalDoakals coach Alan Hatok, who was visibly shaken and searching for answers.

Marvin Harrison took advantage of his opportunities this week with 9 catches for 109 yards and 2 scores. Owners are hoping this is just one of many 100-yard games for Harrison this season. (10/4/2005) — Following the instructions of coach Brian McIntyre to the letter, the Iron City Pounders improved to 2-2 after a solid performance over the slumping Ho Train 94-81.

Brian Griese had a huge game this week with scoring strikes from 41 and 80 yards. Griese turned in his first 300-yard game of the season, but he also had 4 turnovers in the game. Larry Fitzgerald saw a lot of action this week as he caught 7 passes for over 100 yards and a touchdown. It was Fitzgerald's second 100-yard game of the season. Jeremy Shockey found the end zone for the second time this season and finished with 4 catches for 57 yards. Both of Shockey's touchdowns are from 20+ yards out.

"Guys showed that they wanted to keep fighting," Iron City Pounders coach Brian McIntyre said. "All of the negative talk brought us together."

Steven Jackson struggled in the running game, rushing for just 17 yards on 10 carries, but he was able to score on a 1-yard run. Jackson also found the end zone on a 13-yard reception. Edgerrin James just keeps plugging away. James scored on an 8-yard catch this week and delivered his fourth straight game with over 100 yards of total offense. Michael Vick, who saw limited action this week, gained more yards on the ground than he did in the air. Vick salvaged some fantasy value by tossing a 5-yard touchdown pass in the first quarter.

"It kind of stunned me," Ho Train coach Jon Kraus said. "My brain is a little sore, but I'm OK after that. It didn't hinder me at all during the game."
