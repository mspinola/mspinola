(11/1/2005) — Coach Chris Arenth never felt so good going into a game. After a solid week of practice where all of the players finally followed the coach's instructions, the offense clicked from the beginning, scoring 85 points as they improved their record to 5-3 after beating the Da Funk 85-59.

Joey Galloway carried the load again this week with 8 catches for 149 yards and a touchdown. Fortunately for many owners, his 78-yard touchdown provided some big bonus points. Carson Palmer rebounded nicely from last week's disaster with 3 touchdown passes and 237 yards. Palmer, who completed passes to 8 different receivers, leads the league in touchdowns, yards, and completion percentage. Corey Dillon owners should be thankful that Patrick Pass left the game with an injury. Dillon was forced to play through his own injuries and he delivered a 2-touchdown performance.

"What a big win this was for us. The on-field celebration at the end of the game was just to let them know we were here," Carter's Commandos coach Chris Arenth said.

Byron Leftwich turned in decent numbers this week, completing 18 of 31 passes for 213 yards and 2 touchdowns. Leftwich should see some good opportunities next against Houston. Willis McGahee racked up 136 yards on the ground this week, but he didn't see the end zone for the second week in a row. McGahee is second in the league in rushing heading into his bye week.

"We had red zone turnovers. But I'm not going to sit here and tell you, 'If this would have happened, if that would have happened," coach Mark Stahlsmith said. "You saw what happened. They came out and controlled the ballgame."
