(9/27/2005) — Displaying a bad attitude and an endless amount of energy, the Texas Nashvillians laid a fresh beating on the Bunchalosers 143 - 64 behind the cannon arm of Drew Bledsoe, who scored 44 points.

Drew Bledsoe threw for 2 touchdowns and rushed for another as he completed 24 of 38 passes for an outstanding 363 yards in the air. Bledsoe was also picked twice, giving him his first 2 interceptions of the season. For the second week in a row Hines Ward has delivered big. Ward scored from 4 and 85 yards out and turned in his first 100-yard game of the season. Neil Rackers scored all of Arizona's points this week as he booted 4 field goals, 2 of them over 50 yards. Rackers now leads the league in kicking points and is a perfect 10 for 10 on field goals.

"I don't think people expected us to come out and play this well," said Texas Nashvillians coach Randy Folks. "But a lot of guys were wanting to play, just to play."

Marshall Faulk led St. Louis in rushing this week with 50 yards on 6 carries. Faulk also caught 3 passes for 31 yards and a touchdown. Chris Chambers broke out of his fantasy slump this week with 6 catches for 93 yards, including a 42-yard touchdown. It was Chambers first score of the season. Trent Green had a slow start last season and it appears this season is no different. Green delivered a little bit of production this week with 221 yards in the air and a 21-yard touchdown late in the game.

"We know we've got to come out and play 60 minutes," Bunchalosers coach Phil Arrigo said. "We know we can't play for a half or even three quarters. We've got to play the whole game."

Glenshaw (9/27/2005) — The Ho Train beat the Otters 84-75 behind 26 points from WR Chad Johnson in a hotly contested matchup that went down to the wire.

Chad Johnson only caught 3 passes for 77 yards, but he made the most of them. Johnson scored early in the game on an 18-yard catch and then made his day complete with a 40-yarder in the final minutes of the game. Michael Vick surprised most owners this week with his best performance of the season against a tough defense. Vick tossed 2 touchdowns and finished with 167 yards (his season high). Edgerrin James is quietly going about his business. James rushed for his second 100-yard game of the season and scored his first touchdown on a 2-yard run.

"People can criticize," Ho Train coach Jon Kraus said, anticipating surprise at the close game. "But again, you have to capitalize on your chances. One thing people don't realize is that Otters team has some really good players."

Torry Holt lit up the scoreboard with 163 yards receiving including a 25-yard touchdown. Holt is leading the league in receptions and receiving yards. Although owners are getting impatient, Manning is still the most started quarterback in Statsworld leagues.

(9/27/2005) — A series of trick plays created just minutes before the game resulted in a game high 40 points for RB Brian Westbrook as the Karpet Munchers outscored the Iron City Pounders 121-70.

Brian Westbrook was a machine on Sunday, catching 6 passes for 140 yards. Oh yeah, since he's a running back, we'll mention that he also had 68 yards on the ground. Westbrook scored twice for good measure. Touchdown poses aside, Steve Smith was perfect this week. Smith scored 3 touchdowns on 11 receptions and finished with 170 yards. Brett Favre had a lot of success in the first half with 2 touchdown passes, but he struggled mightily in the second half. Favre finished with 195 yards and was picked 3 more times.

"We have a lot of football left, but we're going to be in the hunt for a while," said Karpet Munchers coach John Keenan, showing more emotion than earlier this season. "I think we've got a real shot to do something here."

It looks like Thomas Jones has permanently ended the running back controversy in Chicago after this week's performance. Jones rushed for over 100 yards for the second week in a row and scored his fourth touchdown of the season. Josh Brown finally got an opportunity to do something more than kick extra points. Brown made all 3 of his field goal attempts and added 4 PAT's. Brian Griese wasn't spectacular, but he was good enough to toss 2 touchdown passes. Griese finished with less than 140 yards in the air for the second week in a row.

"We walked out there and we gave them everything we had," Iron City Pounders coach Brian McIntyre said. "We don't apologize to anybody."
