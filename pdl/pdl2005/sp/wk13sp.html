(12/6/2005) — The OakalDoakals and Carter's Commandos entertained the crowds with play after play of highlight-film material.

Anquan Boldin had a huge day with 156 yards receiving, including a 54-yard touchdown and a season high 11 receptions. Boldin hasn't had less than 100 yards in a game since returning from his injury. Domanick Davis rushed for a season high 155 yards and added 16 yards on 3 catches. Davis didn't score this week, but it looks like he will get plenty of touches the rest of the season. Greg Jones accumulated over 100 yards rushing for the second time in the last 4 weeks. If Fred Taylor doesn't play next week Jones could be in for a long day against Indianapolis.

"I remember every game that I ever coached -- all of them," OakalDoakals coach Alan Hatok said. "This is one to remember. It's a big deal that we won. My family and friends are really pumped up that I beat Chris Arenth."

Carson Palmer maintained his touchdown lead by connecting on 3 more scores this week. Palmer completed a season low 58% of his passes for 227 yards and is now second in the league in passer rating. Joey Galloway ended his 2-game scoring slump with a 30-yard touchdown reception. Galloway caught 5 balls for 75 yards. Julius Jones didn't score this week, but it was probably his best all around performance of the season. Jones rushed for 74 yards and caught a season high 9 passes for 88 yards.

"We have to start faster," Carter's Commandos coach Chris Arenth said. "We are digging holes for ourselves and leaving ourselves with problems at the end of games that are hard to overcome."

Pittsburgh (12/6/2005) — Throwing the ball was coach Matt Spinola's number one priority going into the game with the Da Funk and it was executed to perfection as the Manic Moose won 80-52.

Brad Johnson followed up last week's 3-touchdown performance with 2 more touchdowns and 256 yards passing. Johnson appears comfortable with the offense, just in time for a potential shootout next week. Antonio Gates is walking away with the league lead in receptions, yards, and touchdowns among tight ends. Gates scored on a 6-yard catch and had 5 catches for 51 yards this week. Eddie Kennison doesn't catch a lot of passes each week, but he can take advantage of his opportunities. Kennison grabbed 4 balls for 108 yards this week.

"You can grow from slow starts and having to overcome that," Manic Moose coach Matt Spinola said. "This team grew today."

Rudi Johnson pounded out the yardage this week with 98 yards on 21 carries. Johnson also scored 2 touchdowns for the third game in a row. Seattle didn't have much need of Matt Hasselbeck's services this week, limiting him to just 98 yards on 8 of 15 passing. Hasselbeck scored on an 11-yard pass before the game got out of hand.

"We’ll challenge them," Da Funk coach Mark Stahlsmith said. "They’ll find something to moan about, but we’ll challenge the receivers at the same time being smart about it. We’re going to try to be aggressive. If the offense makes a play, so be it. We’re going to get our share of plays, too. Today they made the plays."

Drew Brees bounced back from last week's fantasy disaster with decent numbers… just not enough of them. Brees completed 17 of 22 passes for 160 yards and connected on 2 touchdown passes. (12/6/2005) — Coach Erik Farmerie never felt so good going into a game. After a solid week of practice where all of the players finally followed the coach's instructions, the offense clicked from the beginning, scoring 81 points as they improved their record to 4-9 after beating the Tally Wackers 81-60.

Ben Roethlisberger delivered a game of extremes this week with season highs in touchdowns (3), passing yards (386), completions (29), attempts (41), and unfortunately, interceptions (3).
