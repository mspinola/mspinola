Glenshaw (10/25/2005) — Displaying the physical skills that aren't seen very often, the high-powered Iron City Pounders beat the Otters 112 - 67 in front of a national audience.

With Seattle's high powered offense, Josh Brown has gotten too many field goal opportunities this season. But against a tough defense this week, Brown saw more action and booted 2 field goals from 50+ yards out. Santana Moss has become a fantasy stud. After catching 5 balls for 112 yards and a 32-yard touchdown, he is now atop the leader board in receiving yards with 743. Larry Fitzgerald picked up where he left off before his bye week with his third straight game with a touchdown. This week Fitzgerald caught 4 balls for 71 yards including a 34-yard score.

"We didn't play as well as we have been defensively, but we made the plays when we had to," Iron City Pounders coach Brian McIntyre said. "That's what good teams do."

Mike Anderson averaged 5 yards per carry on his way to 120 yards rushing and a 2-yard touchdown. With Denver it is difficult to predict which running back will be impressive each week. Warrick Dunn still isn't getting many goal line opportunities, but he's still racking up the yardage. On Monday night Dunn rushed for 155 yards on 24 carries is now in second place in the league in rushing yards. It's difficult being the quarterback of the only defeated team in the league when your defense and running game are in a groove. Peyton Manning still isn't close to last year's torrid pace, but he delivered 2 touchdowns 237 yards this week.

"I certainly am in charge of the offense and they weren't in rhythm tonight, but the defense was outstanding," Otters coach Rich Raida said. "There's no excuse for how they played."

Clinton Portis ended his scoring slump in a big way this week. Portis scored on 3 touchdown runs and racked up 101 yards on the ground, his fifth game this season with over 100 yards of total offense. Chris Chambers played almost the entire game with just 1 catch for 11 yards. Then, with less than 3 minutes remaining in the game, Chambers thrilled owners with a 77-yard bomb from the backup quarterback. This week Green threw the ball well, completing 20 of 34 passes for 289 yards, but he was held scoreless for the third time this season.

Kerry Collins played well this week, but he only found the end zone once on a 22-yard strike. Collins, who had his first triple digit passer rating game of the season, completed 19 of 27 passes for 261 yards. Strangely enough, it appears that Marcus Robinson is happy that Nate Burleson is back. Robinson scored his first touchdown of the season on a 27-yard grab and finished with 60 yards. Tiki Barber bounced back from last week's lackluster performance with 110 yards of total offense and a 4-yard touchdown. Although Barber is averaging 4.5 yards per carry he has only turned in one game with 100 yards rushing.

Edgerrin James goes into this week's bye with a league leading 801 yards rushing. James was impressive again this week with 139 yards on 21 carries and 2 more touchdowns. Steven Jackson hammered his way to 97 yards rushing and 2 touchdowns this week. Jackson started the season slowly, but in the past 4 games he has 6 touchdowns and three games with over 100 yards of offense.

"During all of our losses, somehow and someway, we've squandered away the lead," Ho Train coach Jon Kraus said.
