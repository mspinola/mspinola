(11/15/2005) — Making big plays usually isn't a problem for quarterback Kerry Collins and this game was no different as he helped the Scooter's Boozers to a win over the Blitzkrieg 94-52.

Kerry Collins threw his second 300-yard game of the season and found the end zone twice on passing plays. Collins was also intercepted a season high 3 times and was sacked 4 times. Tiki Barber had a spectacular day with 8 receptions for 111 yards, 95 yards on the ground, and a 3-yard touchdown run. Matt Jones saw a lot of action this week and it resulted in 5 catches for a season high 117 yards. Jones also scored his third touchdown of the season on a 32-yard grab.

"We were a sickly team earlier, and became a banged-up team," Scott Arrigo said. "This was one of the reasons I played it very conservatively and safely the last couple of minutes."

Shaun Alexander was incredible again this week, rushing for 165 yards and 3 touchdowns. Alexander leads the league with 17 rushing touchdowns and over 1100 yards on the ground. Donald Driver pulled down a season high 10 balls for 114 yards. Driver hasn't seen the end zone since Week 7, but his increased opportunities is definitely a good sign for owners.

"This was a pretty good beating," Blitzkrieg coach Sean Berecek said. "The score wasn't as bad really as I thought it was. Had we not held them a few times on defense, this could have gotten out of control."

Pittsburgh (11/15/2005) — The Texas Nashvillians faced stronger competition from the hostile fans and slippery field conditions than from the Manic Moose.

Tom Brady tossed 2 touchdowns and threw for 275 yards, but he was also intercepted twice in the game. Going into Monday night's game, Brady was leading the league with over 2500 yards passing. Hines Ward had a big day with 8 catches for 124 yards, including a 51-yard touchdown. Ward's productivity really suffers when Ben Roethlisberger isn't in the game. Larry Johnson owners may be in for some smooth sailing the rest of the season now that Johnson doesn't have to share the ball. Johnson was shutout this week, but he still racked up 178 yards of total offense.

"He's a champion, man," Tom Brady said of coach Randy Folks. "Today coach came out there and had a great performance. No one can out-think our coach."

Once again, Jonathan Wells turned out to be a nice roster replacement for the injured Domanick Davis. Wells scored for the second week in a row, this time on a 14-yard run, and rushed for 58 yards. Brandon Stokley finally got some good opportunities this week and he didn't waste them. Stokley scored his first touchdown of the season on a 21-yard catch and finished with 6 catches for 73 yards. Marty Booker caught 5 passes for over 100 yards for the second time this season. Booker's only score came in Week 1.

"You're right. I didn't prepare well for this matchup," Manic Moose coach Matt Spinola said. "We took the Texas Nashvillians like it was a warmup game, and they took it personal."

It looks like Peyton Manning's fantasy production is getting back to normal. For the second week in a row, Manning tossed 3 touchdowns and just missed another 300-yard game. Antwaan Randle El didn't have much success in the rushing or receiving game this week so he tried his hand throwing the ball. Randle El connected big time on a 51-yard touchdown strike. "Did this pump me up, yeah, a lot. It was very exciting for me," Otters coach Rich Raida said. "It was a little like the good old days where we could call stuff and throw it around, guys making plays, and having fun. These young people coming of age, beginning to come of age, coming together and realizing they could be pretty good, believing they could be pretty good, and then pretty soon you’re pretty good if you’re talented enough."

Samkon Gado was a one man wrecking crew this week as he scored twice on runs and once on a catch. Gado also rushed for over 100 yards for the first time in his very young career. Jake Plummer played really well this week, completing over 70% of his passes for 205 yards and a touchdown, but it was far from his previous game's 4-touchdown performance. Greg Jones did a great job filling in for the injured Fred Taylor. Jones racked up over 100 yards rushing for the first time this season and scored on a 1-yard run.

"I thought our effort was good," OakalDoakals coach Alan Hatok said. "I thought we hustled. I thought we hit. But we made too many mistakes, especially at critical times."
