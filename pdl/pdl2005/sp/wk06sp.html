(10/18/2005) — Playing in front of a standing room only crowd, the Dunbar Dogs folded like a broken chair as the Otters won 71-16, improving to 4-2.

Warrick Dunn looked impressive again this week, scoring on a 21-yard run and rushing for 100 yards. It was Dunn's fifth game this season with over 100 yards of total offense. Pittsburgh (10/18/2005) — The Manic Moose faced stronger competition from the hostile fans and slippery field conditions than from the Beers.

The last time LaDainian Tomlinson threw a touchdown pass (week 3) all he did was score 3 rushing touchdowns to go with it. This week LT mixed it up a bit with passing, rushing, and receiving touchdowns. LT finished with 183 yards of total offense.

"We have some deadly bullets on this team," said coach Matt Spinola, famous for trash-talking opponents each week. "I suggest that there be an investigation, 'cause it looked like there were 12 of us out there."

Aaron Brooks scored two touchdowns in a game for the first time this season on 22 of 33 passing and 259 yards. On the season, Brooks has been sacked 13 times and has 10 turnovers. Kevin Curtis scored his longest touchdown of the season on a wide open 57-yard bomb. Curtis has filled in nicely for Isaac Bruce with back-to-back games with touchdowns.

"I don't see any reason to think that we don't belong in that category," said Beers coach Mike Spinola. "But they earned the right to be where they are, and we have not yet. But we are building something here."

(10/18/2005) — Da Funk emerged the winner in a turf battle royale.

McGahee, averaging over 100 yards rushing per game, is already benefiting from a marginally improved passing game. Shaun Alexander was unstoppable this week as he rushed for 141 yards and 4 touchdowns. The good news for owners is that he has been extremely productive on slightly more than 20 carries per game which should keep his legs fresh the entire season. Donte' Stallworth scored for the first time this season on a 27-yard grab and walked away with 7 balls for 83 yards. Stallworth has been up and down this season, but he could have a big night against a generous St. Louis defense. Drew Brees played well this week, completing 14 of 20 passes for 165 yards and a touchdown, but his fantasy production appears to be stalling out. This week Green completed just 15 passes for 181 yards. Clinton Portis is still waiting for his first score of the season. In the meantime, Portis continues to go about his business collecting yardage. This week Portis rushed for 77 and added 4 catches for 51 yards.

(10/18/2005) — Whether you call them lucky or good, the Iron City Pounders extended their consecutive win streak to 3 games with another good performance, this time at the expense of the Carter's Commandos 117-65.

Santana Moss had an outstanding game with 10 catches, 177 yards, and 2 touchdowns. Entering Monday night's game, Moss was leading the league in receiving yards. Jeremy Shockey proved his new contract wasn't a mistake by catching 5 passes for 129 yards and a touchdown. Shockey is now leading Tight Ends in receiving yards with 380. Curtis Martin tried to do it alone this week and he didn't disappoint. Martin rushed 18 times for 148 yards, his first 100-yard game this season, and scored on a 1-yard run.

"You dream about playing well, but you never know," Iron City Pounders coach Brian McIntyre said. "The main thing is to win. Whether we handed the ball off 50 times or threw the ball, I wanted to win."

Carson Palmer turned in another superb game with 272 yards on 27 of 33 passing and 2 touchdowns. Palmer has completed over 70% of his passes this season and has had a triple digit passer rating in every game. Joey Galloway turned in another good game with 9 catches for 96 yards and a touchdown. Galloway now has 5 scores on the season is averaging just shy of 100 yards per game.

"The performance we put on today was unacceptable," Carter's Commandos coach Chris Arenth said. "I didn't get the message across, but I made it very clear throughout the week that this was a very formidable football opponent."
