Pittsburgh (9/20/2005) — Matt Spinola challenged several players to bring their game to a new level. The inspiring request helped the Manic Moose to improve to 2-0 as they soundly defeated the Carter's Commandos 102-94.

T.O. opened the flood gates this week with 2 long touchdowns and over 140 yards. It looks like Owens is ready to pick up where he left off last season. Ladainian Tomlinson hasn't broken loose for any long runs yet this season, but he's having no problem finding the end zone. L.T. scored on 2 touchdown runs and finished with 52 yards. Willie Parker has no intention of sharing the running duties in Pittsburgh. Parker rushed for over 100 yards again and scored on a 10-yard run.

Matt Spinola, when asked to comment on the team's execution said, "We kept the vision, stayed focused and didn't doubt where we were going."

Carson Palmer looked sharp again as he completed 27 passes to 9 different players. Palmer struck early with a 70-yard touchdown pass and added 2 more scores in the first half. Laveranues Coles scored on a 7-yard catch, but finished with just 5 receptions for 68 yards. At this rate, owners may be waiting for a long time before Coles delivers his first 100-yard game of the season. Julius Jones delivered his second game in a row with over 100 yards of total offense, although he failed to find the end zone this week.

"We're not accomplishing anything we should be," coach Chris Arenth said. "I don't know what it is, but we have to start over, and we have to correct this real fast."

(9/20/2005) — Coach Mark Stahlsmith never felt so good going into a game. After a solid week of practice where all of the players finally followed the coach's instructions, the offense clicked from the beginning, scoring 44 points as they improved their record to 2-0 after beating the OakalDoakals 44-44.

Roy Williams broke free this week on a 51-yard touchdown reception. Williams caught 5 balls for 96 yards. Derrick Mason was contained until the fourth quarter when he scored on a 12-yard catch. Mason finished with 60 yards. Rudi Johnson's load was lighter this week as he carried the ball 22 times and finished with 90 yards. Johnson didn't score in the game.

Plummer finished with 248 yards on 23 of 37 passing.
