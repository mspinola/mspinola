(9/13/2005) — It was an ugly home opener for the Blitzkrieg who spent much of the off season trying to fix two major problems: a defense that allowed too many points and too many yards, and an offense that turned the ball over too frequently.

Randy Moss lived up to all of the hype this week, scoring on a 73-yard reception and finishing with 130 yards. If teams don't double up on him consistently there will be no stopping Moss. Stephen Davis was a pleasant surprise this week, averaging over 6 yards per carry and scoring once. If Davis remains healthy he could have very respectable fantasy numbers. Pittsburgh (9/13/2005) — After spending four quarters trying to give away a victory, the Manic Moose got one anyway -- courtesy of the Scooter's Boozers.

Willie Parker lit up the scoreboard on Sunday with a league leading 161 yards on the ground and over 200 yards of total offense. Parker also scored on an 11-yard run. Courtney Anderson was in the right place at the right time this week. Anderson only caught 3 passes for 18 yards, but 2 of his catches were for touchdowns. Owners can't wait to see Ladainian Tomlinson's role in the passing game increased. LT didn't catch a single pass this week, but he still scraped together 72 yards on the ground and 1 touchdown.

"People can criticize," Manic Moose coach Matt Spinola said, anticipating surprise at the close game. "But again, you have to capitalize on your chances. One thing people don't realize is that Scooter's Boozers team has some really good players."

With a questionable defense and great receivers, Kerry Collins should get a ton of passing opportunities in Oakland this season. Collins tossed 3 touchdowns on 265 yards in the air. Tiki Barber didn't get a lot of touches this week, but he made the most of them. Tiki rushed 13 times for 62 yards, caught 2 passes for 60 yards, and scored on a 21-yard run. Keyshawn Johnson only scored 6 touchdowns all of last season. Keyshawn opened up this season with 2 scores and 65 yards, showing he is still a clutch player.

"I'm not ever going to call my players dumb," Scooter's Boozers coach Scott Arrigo said. "Since you were a kid, your mama taught you not to call anyone dumb or stupid. Maybe I should just say I put the wrong players on the field this week."

Byron Leftwich turned in a nice game to start the season. Leftwich threw for over 250 yards and 2 touchdowns without any turnovers. Rudi Johnson racked up the rushing yards late in the game when the game was out of reach for Cleveland. Johnson finished with 126 yards on the ground and scored on a 1-yard run. (9/13/2005) — Rich Raida challenged several players to bring their game to a new level. The inspiring request helped the Otters to improve to 1-0 as they soundly defeated the Tally Wackers 74-72.

Baltimore's variety of pass coverages stymied Peyton Manning in the first half, but Manning came through in the second half with 2 touchdown passes. Manning finished with 254 yards in the air. Torry Holt picked up where he left off last season with 10 catches for 130 yards. Holt didn't find the endzone, but it looks like he could have another great year. Warrick Dunn didn't score, but he had gaping holes to run through in the rushing department. Dunn average over 5 yards per carry on his way to 117 yards.

"It was personal, very personal," Otters coach Rich Raida said softly. "I spent most of my career competing against the Tally Wackers and I know a lot of people there. It was very personal."

Marc Bulger turned it on late in the game with 2 fourth quarter touchdown passes. Bulger led the league in passing yards (362) and passing attempts (56) this week. Darrell Jackson didn't turn in huge numbers this week, 6 catches for 65 yards, but he scored on 9-yard reception.

"If we didn't get two penalties on third down, it would've been good thinking," Tally Wackers coach Mike Temme said of the strategy. "When you do that, you deserve to lose."

If the first game of the season is any indicator of how Steve Smith will perform this year, he's going to be a fantasy stud. Smith caught 8 passes for 138 yards, a 33-yard touchdown, and had a 70-yarder called back. Marvin Harrison may keep a low profile, but he consistently gets the job done. Harrison scored on a 28-yard reception and finished with 6 catches for 69 yards. Good and bad news for Priest Holmes… he won't have to carry the load by himself. (9/13/2005) — In a typical Alan Hatok-led performance, the OakalDoakals overcame penalties and turnovers to out play the Ho Train 77-46.

Keenan McCardell was like a magnet this week, hauling in 9 passes for 123 yards and 2 touchdowns. McCardell could have a huge season if San Diego is forced to throw the ball a lot this season. Brandon Lloyd is still waiting for the first huge game of his career, but in the meantime he still knows how to find the endzone on limited opportunities. Lloyd caught 3 passes for 65 yards and a score. Jake Plummer struggled all day, completing less than 50% of his passes, throwing 2 interceptions and losing a fumble. Plummer managed to find the end zone on a short pass late in the game.

"It shows you what kind of man he is," OakalDoakals coach Alan Hatok said of Jake Plummer. "He came in in a really tough situation and really took control of the offense and the huddle. He does that every day in practice."

Although Jake Delhomme threw 2 interceptions and lost a fumble, he looked very impressive at times during the game. Delhomme finished with over 200 yards, a 33-yard touchdown, and had a 70+ yard touchdown called back. Sure, Edgerrin James may be one of the best blocking backs in the league, but owners want to see some fantasy production. James finished with over 100 yards of total offense but didn't find the endzone. Chad Johnson didn't score this week, but he made it clear that he will be a force to be reckoned with this season. Johnson caught 8 passes for 91 yards and added 11 yards on the ground.

"We know we've got to come out and play 60 minutes," Ho Train coach Jon Kraus said. "We know we can't play for a half or even three quarters. We've got to play the whole game."
