(11/22/2005) — The Da Funk beat the Tally Wackers 65-61 behind 17 points from RB Rudi Johnson in a hotly contested matchup that went down to the wire.

Rudi Johnson found the end zone twice this week on 1-yard runs and finished with 76 yards on the ground. All five of Johnson's scores this season have come on 1-yard runs. Matt Hasselbeck is one of the best quarterbacks in the league, but his stats don't always reflect it due to Seattle's strong running game. Hasselbeck threw for 233 yards and scored on a 12-yard pass.

"We felt like a kid in a candy store," Da Funk coach Mark Stahlsmith said. "After the first series, we got into a routine and played the game our way."

Marc Bulger was on his way to a great game until he reinjured his shoulder. Bulger completed 19 of 24 passes for 224 yards and 2 touchdowns. Tony Gonzalez is steadily getting back into the heart of the offense. Although Gonzalez didn't score, he caught 9 passes for a season high 98 yards.

"I feel as awful as everyone else does," Mike Temme said. "We have to do way better. We've got to break the skid."

(11/22/2005) — QB Drew Brees scored 51 points to help the Blitzkrieg beat Carter's Commandos 139-85.

Drew Brees put on a great show this week with a season high 4 touchdown passes and 339 yards in the air. Brees was nearly perfect, completing 28 of 33 pass attempts. Donald Driver snagged 2 touchdown passes in the first half and finished with 5 catches for 84 yards. Shaun Alexander continues to be one of the most coveted running backs in the league after scoring 2 more touchdowns and rushing for his seventh 100-yard game. Alexander leads the league in rushing.

"We talked all week about the Carter's Commandos being a turnover team. When they get the ball on turnovers, they've won games," Blitzkrieg coach Sean Berecek said. "When they turn it over, they struggle."

Carson Palmer gave owners his second 300-yard performance of the season, completing 25 of 38 passes for 335 yards. Palmer also lit up the scoreboard with scoring strikes of 68 and 15 yards. Plaxico Burress showed off his speed this week on a 61-yard catch-and-run touchdown. Plaxico caught 6 balls for 113 yards, just his second 100-yard game this season. Julius Jones didn't score this week, but he delighted owners with over 100 yards of total offense. He may give up some carries to Marion Barber, but Jones looked sharp this week.

"We're a young team and have had a lot of frustrations and sometimes they let that frustration get to them and we don't always execute like we're capable of," Carter's Commandos coach Chris Arenth said. "We are improving on that and getting beyond that."

Glenshaw (11/22/2005) — Quarterbacks seem to get all the credit. This game was no different as Aaron Brooks scored 31 points leading the Beers to a 130-125 victory over Otters.

Aaron Brooks delivered his most productive game of the season with 343 yards and 2 touchdowns. Brooks attempted 50 passes in the game. Reuben Droughns got a taste of the end zone last week and liked it (a lot). This week Droughns ripped off a 75-yard touchdown run and finished with an outstanding 166 yards on the ground. For the second time in four weeks Marion Barber scored 2 touchdowns. Although Barber's load decreased with Julius Jones back in the lineup, he still got 15 carries for 53 yards.

"Finally," Beers coach Mike Spinola said. "My guys were really close to landing a victory last week."

Peyton Manning is in the groove. For the third week in a row, Manning threw 3 touchdown passes. This week he also had a season high 365 yards, his second 300-yard game this season. (11/22/2005) — Party time continued for the streaking Karpet Munchers as they beat the Scooter's Boozers to improve their record to 10-1 with 9 wins in a row.

Brett Favre connected on 2 touchdowns in the first half but he was held in check the rest of the game. Favre was also intercepted twice in the game, giving him a league worst 17 picks. Steve Smith became the first wide receiver to surpass the 1,000 yard mark this season, and he did it in a big way. Smith caught a season high 14 passes for 169 yards. No one knew what to expect from Brian Westbrook this week with a new quarterback at the helm, but he still delivered his usual game. Westbrook ran for 66 yards and pulled in 4 balls for 57 yards.

"I'm all right," the Karpet Munchers coach John Keenan said. "Now, tomorrow I might be sore from all of the high-fives. My adrenaline's still flowing, but right now I feel all right. ... Today, I was just in a zone."

Kerry Collins didn't have best stuff this week, but he hooked up on a 49-yard touchdown pass and finished with 289 yards. Tiki Barber didn't get any goal line opportunities again this week, but it didn't slow him down. Barber averaged 5.3 yards per carry on his way to his third 100-yard rushing game of the season.

"I don't think being benched is on his mind right now," Scooter's Boozers coach Scott Arrigo said when asked if the quarterback feared for his starting position. "I've got other problems than who the quarterback is going to be."
