(12/16/2003) — Karpet Munchers may have lost the coin toss, but that is all they lost. They pulverized the Ho Train 153-65 and improved their record to 10-4-1.

Brett Favre turned in his best performance of the season with 278 yards and 4 touchdowns. Favre is now 2nd in the league with 27 passing touchdowns. Brian Westbrook caught 1 touchdown and rushed for another as he finished with over 100 yards of total offense for the 3rd time this season. Westbrook now has 11 touchdowns this season.

"It's one game," Karpet Munchers coach John Keenan said. "One game. You look around the league. Stuff happens in the game. You're with your team. You're with them through the tough things and you're with them through the good times. That's always good to see. I don't think one game should ever determine who comes to the next game."

Terrell Owens crashed through the 1,000-yard mark with 127 yards receiving and a 58-yard touchdown. Owens has scored touchdowns in 4 of his last 5 games. Daniel Graham had his best game of the season as he caught 5 passes for 69 yards and a touchdown.

"We are not retreating," Ho Train coach Jon Kraus said. "We are advancing in another direction."

Pittsburgh, PA (12/16/2003) — LaDainian Tomlinson made running in deep puddles and a steady downpour look easy, successfully cutting from side to side and grinding out yards in the Manic Moose 145-96 victory over the Tally Wackers.

LaDainian Tomlinson had over 140 yards receiving and 2 touchdown catches for the second game in a row. Tomlinson has a league best 2,000 yards from scrimmage by running backs this season. Joe Horn had the game of his life, catching 9 passes for 133 yards and 4 touchdowns. Horn has 10 touchdowns on the season, and will likely break the 1,000-yard mark next week.

"We did a great job answering," Manic Moose coach Matt Spinola said. "We took responsibility for our mistakes and turned it around."

Tony Gonzalez had another big game, catching 6 passes for 93 yards and 2 touchdowns. Gonzalez leads tight ends in receptions, yards, and touchdowns. Marc Bulger threw for 236 yards and 2 touchdowns. Bulger also threw 1 interception and now has 20 on the season.

"That's life," said Tally Wackers coach Mike Temme. "You can't sit and stew about it. That's where we are, and what's important is what we do about it."
