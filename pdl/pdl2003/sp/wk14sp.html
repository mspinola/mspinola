Pittsburgh, PA (12/9/2003)  The Da Funk defensive game plan wasn't enough to slow down the high-powered Manic Moose offense as they got bruised and beaten by point leader LaDainian Tomlinson and his 61 points. The Da Funk record dropped to 6-7-1 after the tough week 14 matchup.

LaDainian Tomlinson had another big game as he caught 9 passes for 148 yards and 2 touchdowns. Tomlinson finished with a season-best 236 yards of total offense. After having 9 turnovers in his previous 4 games, Daunte Culpepper was nearly perfect. Culpepper found holes in the defense all day long as he threw for 274 yards and three 40-yard touchdowns.

"It got pretty scary there toward the end," Matt Spinola said. "There are two types of coaches in this league and today I proved I belong on the winners side."

Fred Taylor had another productive outing, rushing for 163 yards and a touchdown. Taylor has cracked the 100-yard mark in 4 of his last 5 games. Derrick Mason caught 6 passes for 64 yards and 1 touchdown. It was the first time this season that Mason has scored a touchdown in consecutive games.

"We had plenty of meetings, plenty of time for football, plenty of time together. I felt like it would give them a training-camp weekend, where they could think about nothing but the game," Da Funk coach Mark Stahlsmith said. "Obviously, it helped some of us. It didn't help all of us."

"As long as we keep doing what we did today, we can do some pretty special things." Tally Wackers coach Mike Temme said. "This is a special group. I've said that before. We've just got to keep it up."
