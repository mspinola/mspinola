(10/23/2007) — RB LenDale White had a huge game leading the Iron City Pounders to a decisive win over Karpet Munchers 49-45

LenDale White finally cracked the 100-yard barrier this week with 104 yards on 27 carries, 4 receptions for 22 yards, and his fourth score of the season. Jeremy Shockey grabbed five receptions for the fifth time and scored his second touchdown of the season. Shockey finished with 39 yards. With the exception of not losing a fumble this week, Larry Fitzgerald posted identical number as last week: 6 catches for 97 yards and no scores.

"A lot of times I'll put the pressure on myself," Iron City Pounders coach Brian McIntyre said. "I have done that before here and it stirred people up, and I'll take the pressure. I know people have second-guessed me. I don't care. I make decisions based on what gives us the best opportunity to win. If it means I put my own head in the noose, so be it."

Brandon Jacobs turned in his second 100-yard rushing game of the season and scored on a 5-yard run. Jacobs also added 3 receptions for 16 yards. After starting the season on fire, Tony Romo is struggling to turn in another big game. Romo scored once and threw for 277 yards on Sunday. T.J. Houshmandzadeh has become Cincinnati's scoring machine. Housh scored his eighth touchdown of the season but finished with just 4 receptions for 43 yards.

"I made questionable calls all day long. It was pathetic," Karpet Munchers coach John Keenan said. "We'll get it back on track next week."

Glenshaw (10/23/2007) — Making big plays usually isn't a problem for quarterback Peyton Manning and this game was no different as he helped the Otters to a win over the Ho Train 71-37.

Andre' Davis continued his impressive play filling in for the other Andre with a 53-yard touchdown and 88 yards. Torry Holt has only scored once in his past five games, but he's still getting plenty of looks. Holt finished with 8 catches for 82 yards this week.

"From my standpoint, it's fun to watch our offensive line," Otters coach Rich Raida said. "I would hate to be a defensive linemen against our guys. If one of our offensive linemen don't get you, one of our backs will."

Chad Johnson tied his season low in receptions (3) and hasn't scored since week two. Luckily for owners, Chad was able to convert his catches into 102 yards, his third 100-yard game of the season. Jon Kitna had another unproductive game this week with just 147 yards on 16 of 20 passing and no scores.

"I’m not really concerned about that right now," Ho Train coach Jon Kraus said. "I’m just concerned about trying to win a football game and playing a lot better than we have been playing."
