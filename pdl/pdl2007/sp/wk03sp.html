(9/25/2007) — Following the instructions of coach Jon Kraus to the letter, the Ho Train improved to 2-1 after a solid performance over the slumping Tally Wackers 131-85.

Ronnie Brown came out of nowhere to rack up 211 yards of offense and 3 touchdowns. Brown's first two games totaled 141 yards and no scores. Jon Kitna was a yardage machine this week as he completed 29 of 46 passes for 446 yards. Kitna is just shy of 1000 yards after 3 games. Chad Johnson maintained his yardage lead this week by grabbing 9 balls for 138 yards.

"I don't know. I don't know how to answer that," Ho Train coach Jon Kraus said. "When we win a game, we win as a team. When we lose a game, we lose as a team. I'm not ever going to single out one or the other like that, just for future reference."

DeShaun Foster showed some life this week with 122 yards on the ground, one catch for 13 yards, and two scores. Deion Branch scored his first touchdown of the season on a huge 42-yard play. Branch finished with 6 receptions for 77 yards. Jason Witten had a nice game this week with 6 catches for 90 yards and a 3-yard touchdown.

"You mean my scheme?" Tally Wackers coach Mike Temme said, referring to the defense that allowed too many points this week. "I'm not going to get into specifics. It's the same scheme that has been successful in the past."

(9/25/2007) — John Keenan stormed out of the Karpet Munchers locker room before the game with fists pumping and a look of anger as they were getting ready to pound the Carter's Commandos, their most feared opponent.

Tony Romo tossed his second 300 yard game of the season and scored two short touchdowns, keeping him near the top of the QB rankings in most leagues. T.J. Houshmandzadeh was the popular target this week with 12 receptions for 141 yards, including a 35-yard touchdown. LaMont Jordan delivered his third solid performance in a row with 153 yards of total offense and a 1-yard touchdown.

"The sky's the limit for our team," Karpet Munchers coach John Keenan said. "If we can get that through our head, we'll be right where we want to be at the end."

Carson Palmer didn't turn in his most productive game as he completed just 27 of 43 passes for 342 yards, one score, and 2 interceptions. "We needed to execute. We had some plays we didn't get done ... and they did a good job of taking things away," said Carter's Commandos coach Chris Arenth, who was visibly shaken and searching for answers.
