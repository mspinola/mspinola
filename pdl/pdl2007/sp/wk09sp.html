(11/6/2007) — RB Larry Johnson scored 29 points to help the Texas Nashvillians extend their win streak, topping the F-Jackie 108-78.

Larry Johnson continued his steady move up the fantasy leader board with 95 yards of offense, one rushing touchdown, and his first receiving touchdown of the season. Tom Brady doubled his interception total (now has 4) and had his first sub-100 passer rating of the season, but he still delivered a stud-like fantasy performance with 255 yards and 3 scores. Brian Westbrook turned in a fine performance on Sunday with 155 total yards (65 rushing and 14 catches for 90 yards) and a short touchdown.

"Looking back," said coach Randy Folks, "my locker room tirade may have had a positive effect on the team when I started screaming and yelling, guys got behind me. Not that they weren't before, but this was an obvious slap in the face and guys weren't going to put up with that."

Kellen Winslow missed out on a touchdown this week, but he still delivered outstanding stats with a season high 11 catches for 125 yards. Donovan McNabb had an ugly game this week, completing just 27 of 46 passes for 264 yards, two interceptions, and a short score. David Patten scored for the first time this season while pulling in 4 receptions for 81 yards. It looks like Patten's value may be going up now that Brees is back on track.

"This one was tough. We had chances all day," said F-Jackie coach Tony Verdream.

Houshmandzadeh extended his scoring streak to eight games with a 15-yard touchdown on Sunday. Housh didn't have his most productive game with just 4 receptions for 45 yards.
