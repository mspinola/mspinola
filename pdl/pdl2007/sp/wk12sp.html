(11/27/2007) — Whether you call them lucky or good, the Texas Nashvillians extended their consecutive win streak to 8 games with another good performance, this time at the expense of the Scooter's Boozers 79-49.

Tom Brady racked up the yards this week, but delivered his first single touchdown game of the season. Brady completed 34 of 54 passes for 380 yards. Brian Westbrook had a relatively quiet day with just 92 total offensive yards and a 1-yard score. Westbrook leads running backs with over 1450 total yards.

"We're going to learn as a team it's not what you do, it's when you do it," Texas Nashvillians coach Randy Folks said.

(11/27/2007) — Following the instructions of coach Mike Temme to the letter, the Tally Wackers improved to 5-7 after a solid performance over the slumping Dunbar Dogs 49-33.

Greg Jennings is quickly becoming one of the most productive fantasy receivers in the league with two more scores this week. Jennings is now tied for fifth with 9 receiving touchdowns. Eli Manning had a miserable game this week, completing just 21 of 49 passes for 273 yards, one score, and 4 interceptions.

"I've got no reservations about our team," Tally Wackers coach Mike Temme said. "We've got football players. I felt like that all along, or I wouldn't have drafted them. They are all gamers."

Jamal Lewis grounded out the yardage this week with 134 yards on 29 carries. Lewis also scored on a 1-yard run, his seventh score in a row from 1 or 2 yards out. Calvin Johnson had the most productive game of his young career this week with 7 catches for 83 yards and a touchdown. Joseph Addai didn't see the ball much this week, rushing for 44 yards on just 10 carries. Addai also scored once, his eleventh of the season.

"We just had some really costly turnovers at certain points in the game," Dunbar Dogs coach Erik Farmerie said. "But we'll bounce back. We've done it all year."
