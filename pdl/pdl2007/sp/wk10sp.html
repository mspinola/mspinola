(11/13/2007) — It looked like the Karpet Munchers scouts failed to do their jobs as the Texas Nashvillians pulled off a big 119-76 win.

Brian Westbrook was this week's stud running back with a touchdown run and two touchdown receptions. Westbrook rushed for 100 yards and caught 5 balls for 83 yards. Hines Ward pulled in 7 receptions for 80 yards and scored once. Ward is still looking for his first 100-yard game of the season. Jesse Chatman racked up the yardage this week with 124 yards on 27 carries and 5 receptions for 25 yards. If Chatman can start finding the end zone he'll be a solid fantasy producer.

"Our mentality was to come out and prove that last week wasn't a fluke," said Texas Nashvillians coach Randy Folks.

Tony Romo had a huge game this week with 4 touchdowns on 20 of 28 passing for 247 yards. Romo has just one game this season with fewer than 2 scores. Leonard Pope eased some bye week worries on Sunday by grabbing a couple of touchdown passes and finishing with 52 yards. Maurice Morris racked up over 100 yards of offense and scored a touchdown on Monday night. Most of Morris's yardage came late in the game when Seattle had a large lead.

"It's tough, honestly." Karpet Munchers coach John Keenan said. "But you know what, like I told the guys earlier today, if someone is down then someone has to step up and play, which is just how it is. It has been an unusual year that way."

(11/13/2007) — Buddy Verdream is a perfectionist. Even after leading the F-Jackie to a 107-62 win the coach refused to accept any compliments, instead moving on to prepare for the next opponent.

Donovan McNabb had an outstanding game on Sunday with over 250 yards and 4 touchdowns. It was just the second time this season that McNabb found the end zone multiple times in a game. (11/13/2007) — Coach Matt Spinola never felt so good going into a game. After a solid week of practice where all of the players finally followed the coach's instructions, the offense clicked from the beginning, scoring 103 points as they improved their record to 8-2 after beating the Dunbar Dogs 103-76.

Terrell Owens is quickly moving up the leader board after posting his third 100-yard game in a row and scoring twice on Sunday. Owens finished with 6 receptions for 125 yards. As anticipated, Matt Hasselbeck used a much more passing oriented attack on Monday night. Hasselbeck completed 27 of 40 passes for 278 yards and 2 touchdowns. LaDainian Tomlinson failed to gain 100 yards of total offense for the third game in a row, but his touchdown put him in the scoring lead among running backs. LT finished with 97 total yards.

"They did a good job of handling our running game, a lot of times without using their safeties at all," coach Matt Spinola said. "This is one of the best run defenses, if not the best, in our fantasy league. We knew it was going to be tough duty."

Ben Roethlisberger completed 23 of 34 passes for 278 yards and 2 scores, giving him a career high 22 passing TD's this season. Ben also scored his first rushing touchdown of the season on a 30-yard run. Kevin Jones had a strange day… 4 rushing attempts for -4 yards, including a 4 yard touchdown run. Jones also caught 8 balls for 36 yards to finished with positive yardage on the day.

"This was a very disappointing loss for us," Dunbar Dogs coach Erik Farmerie said in a stern voice. "I take full responsibility."
