(10/2/2007) — Party time continued for the streaking Scooter's Boozers as they beat the Bunchalosers to improve their record to 3-1 with 3 wins in a row.

Braylon Edwards made a huge play again this week (78-yard touchdown reception) and finished with 3 catches for 97 yards. Marshawn Lynch is still looking for his first 100-yard game of his career, but he continues to get the job done for owners. Lynch scored on a 10-yard run and finished with 79 yards on 23 carries. Jay Cutler scored two short touchdowns (one on a 2-yard run) but he didn't have much success in the yardage department. Cutler completed 13 of 21 passes for just 131 yards.

"I can't say it turned out like I visualized it would, except I visualized that we would win," Scooter's Boozers coach Scott Arrigo said.

Jerry Porter visited the end zone twice this week, which was really good considering he caught just 3 passes for 52 yards in the game.

"We don't have time to feel sorry for ourselves," Bunchalosers coach Phil Arrigo said. "We have to dig ourselves out of this hole and fight back. We created this situation and we have to be good enough to get out."

(10/2/2007) — Mike Temme executed an offensive strategy that took advantage of each and every weakness of the OakalDoakals defense, improving the Tally Wackers record to 2-2.

Dwayne Bowe had an outstanding game with 8 catches for 164 yards and a 51-yard touchdown. Bowe scored for the third game in a row. Jason Witten was a frequent target for Tony Romo this week as he grabbed 6 balls for 71 yards and his third touchdown of the season. Deion Branch couldn't find the end zone this week, but it didn't stop him from racking up some yardage. Branch caught 7 balls for 130 yards, his second 100-yard game of the season.

"We were sputtering so bad on offense and misfiring, and we had to make a play. I knew if we were going to win the game, we had to make one of those plays," Tally Wackers coach Mike Temme said.

Travis Henry is leading the league in rushing, but owners need him to find the end zone more often. Henry rushed for 131 yards this week, but didn't score for the third time this season. Edgerrin James did okay against a tough Pittsburgh defense this week. James rushed for 77 yards and scored on a 2-yard run.

"These guys have been working their tails off," OakalDoakals coach Alan Hatok said. "The veterans have been excellent. The effort has been good. Right now, we're just not putting together a complete game."

(10/2/2007) — The high scoring Dunbar Dogs had no problem finding the end zone against the very generous Carter's Commandos defense. Head coach and defensive coordinator Chris Arenth toyed with several different schemes before settling on the prevent defense early in the 3rd quarter.

Santonio Holmes came alive this week with two touchdowns and 6 catches for 128 yards, his first 100-yard game of the season. Joseph Addai has been the most consistent producer among running backs this season. Addai scored for the fourth game in a row and racked up a season high 136 yards on the ground. Ben Roethlisberger connected on a couple of touchdown passes and threw for 244 yards, but his day wasn't completely pleasant. Roethlisberger was sacked 4 times and picked twice.

"I thought our team came out and played physical today," Dunbar Dogs coach Erik Farmerie said. "We wanted to be physical from the onset. I thought our units were cohesive. Our run defense was outstanding, and we limited Carter's Commandos a great deal."

Julius Jones got a few more touches this week and was able to turn them into a 2-yard touchdown and 52 yards rushing. Laveranues Coles caught a season high 8 balls for 65 yards and scored his fourth touchdown of the season. Carson Palmer threw 2 interceptions for the third game in a row and wasn't very productive with just one short score and 230+ yards.

"The worst thing is to have a guy come out and shoot his mouth making a statement about guaranteeing the victory, and they stood up to it," Carter's Commandos coach Chris Arenth said. "They are a good team, but we feel like we shouldn't have lost."
