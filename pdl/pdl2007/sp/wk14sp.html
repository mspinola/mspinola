(12/11/2007) — Whether you call them lucky or good, the Beers extended their consecutive win streak to 4 games with another good performance, this time at the expense of the Karpet Munchers 75-37.

Experts predicted a big game from Marion Barber this week and for once they were right. Barber had two rushing touchdowns, a receiving touchdown, 10 receptions, and over 100 total yards. Randy Moss got back to business this week with a huge game. Moss, who caught 7 balls for 135 yards and scored twice, has a five touchdown lead among wide outs on the season. Derek Anderson had another solid outing this week, but he hasn't been able to repeat his early season magic. Anderson had two short touchdowns and finished with 185 yards.

"I just got into a groove and I just kept wanting the ball," said Marion Barber, who received the game ball from coach Mike Spinola. "He didn't want to give it up to anybody else so I'm letting him take it home," joked Mike Spinola.

Tony Romo finished strong this week with a game winning touchdown at the end of the game and over 300 yards passing. Romo has thrown for 35 scores against 12 interceptions this season.

"In a minute-forty we had a dropped touchdown, two fumbles, a blocked punt, two penalties, and missed tackles for a touchdown," Karpet Munchers coach John Keenan said. "Twenty-eight minutes before that we played good football. I lost my appetite Sunday night."

Pittsburgh (12/11/2007) — Quarterbacks seem to get all the credit. This game was no different as Matt Hasselbeck scored 42 points leading the Manic Moose to a 151-86 victory over Blitzkrieg.

Matt Hasselbeck has been very consistent this season, so it came as a pleasant surprise when he tossed 4 touchdown passes this week. Hasselbeck completed 22 of 33 passes for 272 yards in the game. LaDainian Tomlinson racked up the yards this week with 146 yards on 26 carries, 5 receptions for 27 yards, and two touchdowns. LT is poised to take over the rushing lead with one more good game. Earnest Graham has become an outstanding and consistent fantasy producer. This week Graham rushed for 61 yards, caught 6 balls for 45 yards, and scored twice.

"It was personal, very personal," Manic Moose coach Matt Spinola said softly. "I spent most of my career competing against the Blitzkrieg and I know a lot of people there. It was very personal."

Drew Brees had an outstanding game on Monday night, completing 28 of 41 passes for 328 yards and 3 touchdowns. Brees has connected for 9 scores in his last 3 games, but he has just 300 yard games this season. Reggie Williams hasn't convinced most owners that he is the real deal yet, but he had another nice game this week. Williams scored on a 22-yard reception and finished with 90 yards.

"It's obvious this ballgame flipped in the third quarter," Blitzkrieg coach Sean Berecek said. "This was very disappointing coming off two wins and a close game at halftime. Two possessions and a punt return and it's a completely different game."

(12/11/2007) — Coach Erik Farmerie never felt so good going into a game. After a solid week of practice where all of the players finally followed the coach's instructions, the offense clicked from the beginning, scoring 103 points as they improved their record to 7-7 after beating the F-Jackie 103-93.

Jamal Lewis continued his spectacular second half turnaround with two more touchdowns and 137 yards of total offense. Lewis has four 100 yard games and 9 touchdowns in his last six games. Fred Taylor gave owners three solid games in a row, this week with 132 rushing yards, 2 catches for 24 yards, and a huge 80-yard touchdown run. Taylor has rushed for over 100 yards in 3 games in a row now. Roddy White grabbed just 3 catches on Monday night, but one of them was good for a score. White finished with 75 yards.

"We watched the film," Dunbar Dogs coach Erik Farmerie said. "The running backs all had a good game. We thought we could do some good things against them because of the size of our offensive line."

Brandon Marshall had his best game of the season with over 100 yards and two scores. It was Marshall's first multi-touchdown game and second 100 yard performance of the season. Jabar Gaffney was this week's surprise performer, grabbing 7 receptions for 122 yards and a 56-yard score. Ryan Grant looks more like a feature back every time he plays. Grant rushed for 156 yards on 29 carries and scored once this week, giving him 3 solid outings in a row.

"I'm not going to get on my players any more this season," F-Jackie coach Buddy Verdream said. "We are what we are."

(12/11/2007) — Undaunted by the storied stadium and fans, QB Peyton Manning made history of his own as he led the Otters to victory with 47 points.

It looks like Peyton Manning may be peaking just in time for the fantasy playoffs. Manning had his best game of the season on Sunday with 4 scores from 19+ yards out and 249 yards on 13 of 17 passing. Marques Colston had an outstanding game on Monday night with 9 receptions for 92 yards and 2 touchdowns. Colston has had six very productive games in his last seven outings. Shayne Graham booted four more field goals this week to help Cincinnati bring home the win. Graham accounted for 13 of the team's 19 points.

"It wasn't one of our best performances, but anytime you can get a win, especially in the situation that we're in, it does mean a lot," said Otters coach Rich Raida.

Tom Brady ended his 2 game slump with an outstanding performance against a tough Pittsburgh defense. Brady completed 32 of 46 passes for 399 yards and 4 scores (two of them longer than 55 yards). Brian Westbrook must be the most quiet top notch running back in the league. Westbrook, who rushed for 116 yards and caught 5 balls for 38 yards and a touchdown, now leads running backs with 1752 offensive yards. Tony Gonzalez had another outstanding game this week with his fifth score of the season on 7 receptions for 76 yards. Gonzalez is one reception and one yard shy of the league lead in those categories.

"Having a solid offensive lineup clearly gives us a chance, but we have some young people in there," Texas Nashvillians coach Randy Folks said. "When we had chances at plays, crazy things happened."
