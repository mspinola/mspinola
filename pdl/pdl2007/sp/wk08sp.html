(10/30/2007) — After hours of stadium preparation, the F-Jackie made sure the Bunchalosers felt welcome when they arrived. But the welcome wagon left early in the game as the F-Jackie dominated in a 87-21 win.

Reggie Wayne was on fire this week with Marvin Harrison out of the lineup. Wayne caught 7 balls for 168 yards including a 59-yard touchdown. (10/30/2007) — Showing good team chemistry, the Tally Wackers outscored the Iron City Pounders 76-75. "Eli Manning knows what I can do," Greg Jennings said. "He goes through his reads and if I'm open, he will throw the ball in my direction."

Greg Jennings gave owners a treat on Monday night with an 82-yard touchdown on the first play in overtime. Jennings finished with 6 receptions for 141 yards. Eli Manning must have been suffering from jet lag this week as he completed just 8 of 22 passes for 59 yards. Manning failed to throw a touchdown for the first time this season, but did score on 10-yard run. DeShaun Foster scored for the first time since week 3, but his yardage numbers are still suffering. Foster hasn't rushed for more than 64 yards in any of his last four games.

"This was a great win for Tally Wackers football," coach Mike Temme said. "I've coached for a while now and I've never been more proud of my roster and lineup."

Not surprisingly, Brett Favre rose to the occasion on Monday night throwing for over 300 yards and two huge touchdowns. It was Favre's fourth 300 yard game of the season. LenDale White must finally be getting into shape. White rushed for a career high 133 yards on 25 carries, his second 100-yard game in a row. Thomas Jones caught a season high 5 balls for 34 yards and rushed for 70 yards to finish with his third 100-yard game of the season.

"Brett Favre has had better games, but they put a lot of pressure on him," Iron City Pounders coach Brian McIntyre said.

(10/30/2007) — Peyton Manning might not get the attention he deserves but he sure received attention from those in attendance at the stadium after scoring 28 points in leading the Otters to a come from behind win over the Carter's Commandos 66 - 47.

Peyton Manning got off to a very slow start on Sunday but rebounded nicely in the second half with a couple of touchdowns. Manning finished with 254 yards on 14 of 30 passing. Torry Holt turned in his first 100-yard game of the season with 6 receptions for 110 yards. Holt also scored on 1-yard reception, his fourth score this season. Selvin Young got the carries for Denver on Monday night, rushing for 71 yards and catching 5 balls for 71 yards.

"There was a lot of talk this week whether I should make a change at quarterback. I've said all along I don't care what a lot of people on the outside think, but I know these guys in this locker room believe in me and I think I choose the right starter, Otters coach Rich Raida said. Hopefully, today maybe I proved a point."

Joey Galloway had another long touchdown reception this week while grabbing 6 receptions for 115 yards. Three of Galloway's four scores are from over 50 yards out. Carson Palmer didn't have a bad day; he was just unable to make any big plays on Sunday. Palmer completed 23 of 31 passes for 205 yards and a short touchdown.

"I should be disappointed, and I am in a certain way -- but these guys in this room were fighting all the way to the last second," Chris Arenth said. "We just can't spot a team that many points. They made the plays. We had a good scheme, but it didn't work against them."

Lee Evans finally delivered what owners have been expecting from him… a long touchdown reception. Evans grabbed 5 balls for a season high 138 yards.
