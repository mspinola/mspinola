Pittsburgh (12/18/2007) — After hours of film study, the Manic Moose crafted a plan that handled everything the Dunbar Dogs could throw at them, winning 80-71 and improving their record to 11-4.

LaDainian Tomlinson ran wild on Sunday before being pulled due to San Diego's huge lead. Tomlinson scored twice and rushed for 116 yards on 15 carries. Matt Hasselbeck had a relatively quiet game this week with just one score and 274 passing yards. Nate Kaeding had his most productive game of the season with 3 field goals and 6 extra points. Kaeding is tied for 25th with just 18 made field goals this season.

"We're definitely happy with the win, but we're not satisfied," Manic Moose coach Matt Spinola said. "We know we have to stay poised for our next game. We feel we're a different football team now than in years past. We think that's going to lead to playoff success."

Ben Roethlisberger had decent fantasy stats this week, primarily due to Pittsburgh playing from behind the entire game. Roethlisberger tossed 3 touchdowns, but finished with just 146 yards. Jamal Lewis pounded out the yardage in the snow on Sunday, gaining 163 yards on 33 carries and catching 1 ball for 11 yards. Calvin Johnson didn't score this week, but he did grab 5 receptions for 102 yards, the first 100-yard game of his career.

The great coaching experiment for the Dunbar Dogs endured another harsh blow, certainly leading to more fan hate mail. "I'm running out of excuses," said Erik Farmerie. "We need to catch a break."

(12/18/2007) — When the going got tough Otters coach Rich Raida got tougher, leading the team to 62-61 victory over the Beers.

Marques Colston continued his outstanding second half with over 100 yards and another score. Colston's 8 receptions for 114 yards pushed him over 1000 yards on the season. Peyton Manning connected on just one touchdown pass this week and threw for 276 yards with one interception. Manning has a touchdown pass in every game this season. Torry Holt scored on a 4-yard reception, but that's about all he had going on this week. Holt finished with 3 catches for 21 yards.

"You have got to win at least two of the three - offense, defense and special teams - and we did that," Otters coach Rich Raida said. "We came out on offense and got down in the red zone and were able to put points on the board."

Adrian Peterson was the only offensive player to produce meaningful stats on Monday night, scoring a couple of touchdowns in the second half and finishing with 95 yards of total offense. Chris Henry made his only reception count this week… a 52-yard touchdown. Henry hasn't come close to a 100-yard game since his 99 yard performance in week ten. Derrick Mason pulled in 4 balls for 45 yards and a score in Baltimore's disappointing loss to previously winless Miami. Mason is third in the league with 92 receptions.

"We’ll challenge them," Beers coach Mike Spinola said. "They’ll find something to moan about, but we’ll challenge the receivers at the same time being smart about it. We’re going to try to be aggressive. If the offense makes a play, so be it. We’re going to get our share of plays, too. Today they made the plays."
