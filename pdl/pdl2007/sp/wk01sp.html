(9/11/2007) — The Tally Wackers beat the Carter's Commandos 73-52 behind 20 points from TE Jason Witten in a hotly contested matchup that went down to the wire.

Jason Witten could have a huge season if Romo keeps targeting him like he did on Sunday. Witten pulled in 6 balls for 116 yards and a touchdown. Ben Roethlisberger evidently doesn't want a repeat of last season's struggles. Roethlisberger passed for just 161 yards, but 4 of his 12 completions were for touchdowns. Joseph Addai scared owners early with a potential injury, but came back strong with 118 yards on 23 attempts and 3 catches for 25 yards. Pittsburgh (9/11/2007) — It looked like the Da Funk scouts failed to do their jobs as the Manic Moose pulled off a big 152-65 win.

Plaxico Burress had a monster game this week with 3 scores and 144 yards on 8 receptions. LaDainian Tomlinson had an impossible day on the ground (17 rushes for 25 yards and a touchdown), but he kept owners happy with a 17-yard touchdown pass and 7 catches for 51 yards. Terrell Owens had a slow start this week, but he didn't let that stop him from having a big game. Owens grabbed 3 balls for 87 yards, two of them for touchdowns.

Frank Gore didn't have his most productive game (55 rushing yards and 1 reception for 21 yards), but his 6-yard touchdown certainly helped. Roy Williams grabbed a 13-yard touchdown pass, but wasn't able to get much going otherwise. Williams other 3 receptions were for a total of just 7 yards.

Glenshaw (9/11/2007) — It was an ugly home opener for the Otters who spent much of the off season trying to fix two major problems: a defense that allowed too many points and too many yards, and an offense that turned the ball over too frequently.

Jon Kitna found the end zone three times while completing 27 of 36 passes for 289 yards. Kitna also got picked a couple of times. Andre Johnson started his season in style with a huge 77-yard touchdown reception. Johnson finished with 7 catches for 142 yards. Chad Johnson got on the scoreboard early with a 39-yard touchdown reception. Johnson finished with 5 catches for 95 yards.

"I am really proud of the way that Jon Kitna handled the game and also the way the players around him stepped up, which is what has to happen," Ho Train coach Jon Kraus said. "This was a huge comeback for us and a huge character game."

Peyton Manning picked up where he left off last season with 3 touchdown passes, 288 yards, and no turnovers. Torry Holt didn't have a huge game, but it was nice to see some productivity from him after all of the injury talk. Holt caught 8 balls for 73 yards and a short touchdown.
