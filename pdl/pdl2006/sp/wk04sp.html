(10/3/2006) — The Texas Nashvillians beat the Scooter's Boozers 79-41 behind 22 points from RB Larry Johnson in a brutal rivalry.

Larry Johnson continued to rack up the yards this week with 142 total yards and he gave owners a bonus with his first 2 touchdowns of the season. Drew Bledsoe's skills weren't in high demand in the blowout against Tennessee this week, but he made the most of it with 2 touchdowns and 179 yards on 13 of 20 passing. Chris Chambers must be frustrated with how this season has started. Although Chambers scored on a 16-yard reception, he is still looking for his first game with over 59 yards receiving.

Jeff Wilkins booted 4 more field goals this week (with one miss) and is now 15 of 18 on the season. Wilkins has made more field goals than any other kicker has attempted through week 4.

"It's tough, honestly." Scooter's Boozers coach Scott Arrigo said. "But you know what, like I told the guys earlier today, if someone is down then someone has to step up and play, which is just how it is. It has been an unusual year that way."

Pittsburgh (10/3/2006) — QB Byron Leftwich had a huge game leading the Da Funk to a decisive win over Manic Moose 89-74

Roy Williams had another big game with 9 receptions for 139 yards. Although Williams didn't score this week, it's obvious he has become Kitna's favorite target. (10/3/2006) — After hours of stadium preparation, the Tally Wackers made sure the Dunbar Dogs felt welcome when they arrived. But the welcome wagon left early in the game as the Tally Wackers dominated in a 114-32 win.

Marc Bulger followed up last week's 300-yard performance with another one this week. Sunday, Bulger connected on 3 touchdown passes and threw for 328 yards. Reggie Williams continue to surprise many owners as he keeps inching his way up the leader board. On Sunday Williams grabbed 2 touchdown receptions and finished with 93 yards. DeShaun Foster looked good this week with 105 yards on 16 carries, including a 43-yard touchdown. It was Foster's first 100-yard game of the season, securing his role as the feature back for now.

"That's having my back," said Tally Wackers coach Mike Temme, who apologized to some of the Dunbar Dogs on the field for the victory guarantee. "They didn't leave me hanging in the wind, and I didn't want to leave them hanging in the wind."

Joseph Addai just missed his second 100-yard game of the season with 84 yards rushing and 15 yards on 3 catches. Addai got 20 carries to Dominic Rhodes' 15 carries this week.

"Nobody has surrendered yet," Dunbar Dogs coach Erik Farmerie said. "We're trying to keep the pride. It's just getting hard right now."
