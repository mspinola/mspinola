(10/17/2006) — The Da Funk rested their starters for most of the second half as they throttled the Scooter's Boozers 101-22 and extended their winning streak to 3 games.

Apparently the bye week was exactly what Matt Hasselbeck needed to get over his week 4 disaster. Hasselbeck threw for a season high 268 yards and scored 3 touchdowns. Pittsburgh (10/17/2006) — The Manic Moose offensive line dominated the trenches, opening up holes that LaDainian Tomlinson could have driven a truck through, but he decided to run while racking up 35 points.

LaDainian Tomlinson rose to the top this week with 4 touchdowns and 135 total yards. Now that San Diego's offense appears to be clicking owners could see a lot more of this from L.T. Josh Brown hasn't been very accurate this season, making only 8 of 12 field goal attempts, but he came through this week with two 49-yard field goals and a 54-yard game winner. "I had confidence before this game," said winning coach Matt Spinola. "What I got from this game was experience. It was huge."

(10/17/2006) — The Iron City Pounders were back to their old tricks -- a field goal snafu, special team shenanigans, and an offense that was humbled early and often as they lost to Carter's Commandos 54-39.

Carson Palmer struggled to find the end zone again this week, but struck pay dirt on a perfect pass late in the second quarter. Palmer threw for over 250 yards for just the second time this season. Julius Jones rushed for over 100 yards for the third week in a row, moving him within 39 yards of the league lead. Jones has just 2 receptions in 5 games this season.

"The big thing with our game last week was that we didn't finish what we started," Carter's Commandos coach Chris Arenth said. "This week, we knew that we had to come out and finish the job if we wanted to win. It motivated us all week in practice and our intensity was very high all week."

Jeremy Shockey wasn't worth mentioning the previous 4 weeks, but that isn't the case this week. Shockey scored twice and finished with 6 catches for 55 yards.

"Maybe I'm struggling calling plays right now. Shoot, it's not working as well as it should," said Iron City Pounders coach Brian McIntyre, who handed the duties to a co-worker. "I was asked to give up some of my responsibilities as coach and I said 'maybe'. ... I have to bench the play-caller, then I can do that. I benched myself a little bit."
