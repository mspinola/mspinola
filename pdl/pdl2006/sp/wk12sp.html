Pittsburgh (11/28/2006) — Focused more on winning than avoiding mistakes , the Manic Moose (8-4) turned in several big plays in an easy win over the Blitzkrieg (5-7).

LaDainian Tomlinson must get tired of scoring all those rushing touchdowns; maybe that's why he threw his second touchdown pass of the season. LT finished with over 100 yards of total offense and 3 scores. Antonio Gates grabbed 6 balls for 81 yards this week, but his favorite play of the day came on a pass from LaDainian Tomlinson that resulted in a 19-yard score. Terrell Owens had one of his best games of the season with 8 catches for 107 yards and a score. Entering Monday's game, Owens is tied for the lead in receiving touchdowns, but has just two 100-yard games.

"We're in a good spot. Not only are we winning, we're pretty injury-free," Matt Spinola said. "Blitzkrieg, if you look at it, should have been hungrier than they were."

Drew Brees got off to a great start with 2 long touchdowns in the first, but was held scoreless the rest of the way. Brees finished with 349 yards, his fifth 300+ yard game in a row. It appears that Shaun Alexander has officially shaken off the cob webs after he ran the ball 40 times for 201 yards on Monday night. Maybe next week Alexander will resume his end zone visits. Donald Driver caught just 2 passes this week and one of them resulted in a 48-yard score near the beginning of the second half. Driver finished with 82 receiving yards.

"We didn't have the goal-line offense today," Blitzkrieg coach Sean Berecek said. "Everybody was in street clothes over there."

(11/28/2006) — Coach Mark Stahlsmith never felt so good going into a game. After a solid week of practice where all of the players finally followed the coach's instructions, the offense clicked from the beginning, scoring 113 points as they improved their record to 9-3 after beating the F-Jackie 113-64.

Matt Leinart struggled to find the end zone this week, but owners got their money's worth out of him. Leinart completed 31 of 51 passes for the first 400-yard game of his career. Frank Gore has turned into a real yardage machine. Gore gained 134 yards on the ground and 31 yards on 4 catches to keep him within 25 yards of the rushing title. Roy Williams failed to score for the third week in a row, but it didn't stop him from racking up 126 yards on 6 receptions.

"At halftime, I was very emotional in the locker room," Da Funk coach Mark Stahlsmith said. "I care about the team, and I felt I had let people down after my starters got off to a slow start."

David Carr's only score this week came on a 3-yard completion, but he racked up the passing yardage in the process. Carr completed 39 of 54 passes for his first 300-yard game of the season. Brian Westbrook isn't letting anything slow him down on his way to an outstanding season. Westbrook scored on a 6-yard run and racked up 170 yards of total offense. Reggie Wayne suffered as a result of Philadelphia's lousy rush defense this week and didn't see many opportunities. Wayne still managed to pull in 4 balls for 77 yards and a score.

"We want to go out and play hard every game and see how it goes from there," F-Jackie coach Buddy Verdream barked. "We need to put this loss behind us and learn from our mistakes. We would like to win out from here."
