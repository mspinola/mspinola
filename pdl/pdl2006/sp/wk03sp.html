Glenshaw (9/26/2006) — Quarterbacks seem to get all the credit. This game was no different as Peyton Manning scored 26 points leading the Otters to a 92-57 victory over Ho Train.

Peyton Manning started slowly, but he posted decent numbers in the second half with a couple of touchdowns. Manning finished with 219 yards on just 14 completions. Torry Holt is finally getting into a groove. This week Holt delivered his first 100-yard game of the season and scored on a 9-yard reception. Robbie Gould repeated his week 1 success by making all 4 of his field goal attempts, giving him 10 on the season. Gould now leads the league in kicking points.

"I think we're starting to develop and believe in ourselves," said Otters coach Rich Raida. "We've got the talent. We've got the ability to play with anybody in the league."

Andre Johnson was a stud this week as he grabbed 11 balls for 152 yards. Entering Monday's game, Johnson was ranked third in receptions and receiving yards. Steven Jackson kept his streak alive with 120+ yards of total offense in each game this season. Jackson's other streak (no touchdowns this season) is one owners hope will end soon. Lee Evans finally showed up this week after 2 dismal weeks to open the season. Evans pulled in 8 receptions for 107 yards on Sunday.

"It's not the end of the world, but it deflates you a little bit," Ho Train coach Jon Kraus said. "The plays were there to be made. The ball bounces your way sometimes, and sometimes it doesn't. We were on the wrong side of a fine line today."

Pittsburgh (9/26/2006) — Matt Spinola acknowledged being nervous before the game, partly because of the outlandish “taking candy from a baby” comment on how the Manic Moose would have their way against the Blitzkrieg defense.

Willie Parker bounced back strongly from last week's poor showing with 133 yards on the ground and 2 short touchdowns. Daunte Culpepper continued to depress owners who took a chance on him. Culpepper, who was sacked 5 times, scored on a 5-yard run and threw for 168 yards.

"We moved into our quick attack offensive set that we haven't played since training camp," Manic Moose coach Matt Spinola said. "Our guys were smart enough that when I pulled it out of our backup bag, they knew what I was talking about."
