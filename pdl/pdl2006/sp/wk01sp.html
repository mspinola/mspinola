(9/12/2006) — A strong exhibition season is no guarantee of success. The Carter's Commandos, unbeaten in the preseason, quickly lost their momentum in the season opener against the Iron City Pounders.

Jeremy Shockey came alive in the second half and found the end zone on a 15-yard catch. Shockey finished with 5 receptions and 59 yards. Larry Fitzgerald picked up right where he left off last season with 9 receptions and 133 yards. If Fitzgerald stays healthy he could very well dominate the position like Steve Smith did last season. Muhsin Muhammad was held scoreless, but he managed to grab 6 balls for his first 100-yard game of the season.

"We hope winning these close games is a big building block," Iron City Pounders coach Brian McIntyre said. "Every week in this league, most games come down to the wire. Our guys made the plays they needed to make to win."

Julius Jones looked good in the opener, scoring on a 23-yard run and racking up over 100 yards of offense. Plaxico Burress began the season on the right tone, scoring on a 34-yard catch on 4 catches for 80 yards. Antonio Bryant benefited from Alex Smith's improved play by catching 4 passes for 114 yards.

"I have a short memory," Carter's Commandos coach Chris Arenth said of Carson Palmer. "And he should be happy about it."

Pittsburgh (9/12/2006) — After a great mini-camp and a strong preseason, the Manic Moose appeared to have been confused with everything from the shape of the football to the referee's whistle.

Kurt Warner was impressive in the opener, throwing for over 300 yards and scoring 3 touchdowns. If he remains healthy Warner could have a huge season. Jeff Wilkins started the season on a high note by scoring every point for St. Louis as they upset Denver. Wilkins made 6 of 7 field goals in the game. (9/12/2006) — The offseason was unkind to the Texas Nashvillians. So they took out all of their pent up frustration on the Da Funk in their impressive 88-59 win.

Tony Gonzalez rebounded from last season's productivity shortage with 10 catches for 81 yards and a touchdown. Larry Johnson racked up the yardage (68 yards rushing and 80 yards receiving), but he was held scoreless. It will be interesting to see how Johnson does on the ground with KC's questionable line. Shane Graham picked up where he left off last season with 3-for-3 on field goal attempts and 2 extra points.

Texas Nashvillians coach Randy Folks and quarterback Tom Brady were ecstatic with their 88-59 victory over the Da Funk. "We were talking about our fine overall play," Randy Folks said with a wink. "We were both darn proud of our performance."

Frank Gore delivered an awesome performance in his first game as the undisputed feature back in San Fran. Gore rushed for 80+ yards, caught 6 passes for 80+ yards and scored on 2 short runs. Rudi Johnson gave Cincinnati a boost with a 22-yard touchdown run and 96 yards rushing. Johnson added a reception for 7 yards to get his first 100-yard game of the season. Alge Crumpler caught just 3 balls for 27 yards but he found the end zone on a 2-yard reception.

"We didn't play the way I wanted us to play," said Da Funk coach Mark Stahlsmith, who still expressed surprise at the loss.

(9/12/2006) — In a typical Sean Berecek-led performance, the Blitzkrieg overcame penalties and turnovers to out play the Beers 84-55.

Chad Pennington may have thrown 2 interceptions, but it was his best fantasy production in ages. Chad threw for over 300 yards and 2 touchdowns. It looks like Donte' Stallworth will fit right in at Philadelphia. Stallworth's debut was outstanding with a 42-yard score and 141 yards receiving. John Kasay demonstrated his skills in kicking long field goals by scoring from 46 and 54 yards out.

"I felt like they could only contain us for so long, and we would find a way to make some plays," Blitzkrieg coach Sean Berecek said. "It speaks for how we stick together, how we made some plays in key situations."

Adam Vinatieri appears to be comfortable in his new home as he made all 4 of his field goal attempts and 2 extra points. Fred Taylor accumulated over 100 yards of total offense and scored on a 5-yard run. If he can keep this up AND stay healthy he will make some owners very happy this season. Terry Glenn grabbed 4 balls for 81 yards, but you have to wonder how often he will see the end zone with TO in the mix at Dallas.

"It wasn't a matter of confusion," said Beers coach Mike Spinola, who fell to 0-1. "We knew what was coming, we knew what they were doing. ... I'm frustrated with our production. I have complete confidence in my ability to coach the game."

(9/12/2006) — Relying upon an all-star performance from Peyton Manning, who scored 15 points, the Otters grabbed a decisive victory of the Tally Wackers 41-35.

Peyton Manning didn't start the season on fire, his only score coming on a 2-yard pass. Peyton completed just 25 of 41 passes for 276 yards. Entering Monday night's game, Warrick Dunn led the league in rushing by gaining 132 yards on 29 carries in the season opener. Torry Holt saw a lot of action this week, catching 7 balls for 80 yards. Unfortunately Holt was held scoreless.

"Once we made a couple of plays in the passing game, Peyton Manning got going a little more and everyone got hyped up," said Otters coach Rich Raida.

Eli Manning didn't bring home the family trophy this week, but his numbers sure didn't support that. Eli tossed 2 touchdowns and threw for just shy of 250 yards. Tatum Bell looked excellent in the season opener with 103 yards on 15 rushing attempts. Until a clear feature back emerges, maybe Mike and Tatum will both be starting in most leagues. DeShaun Foster rushed for 54 yards and added 20 more on 4 receptions. But take heart, Carolina's entire offense appeared to be in a funk this week.

"I'm just happy to be where we are now," Tally Wackers coach Mike Temme said. "Hey, we're going to have to play a lot better than we did today if we're going to do something."

(9/12/2006) — It was an ugly home opener for the OakalDoakals who spent much of the off season trying to fix two major problems: a defense that allowed too many points and too many yards, and an offense that turned the ball over too frequently.

Steven Jackson was held scoreless this week, but it wasn't due to being unprepared for the new season. Jackson was impressive as he rushed for 121 yards and caught 4 balls for 19 yards. Edgerrin James wasn't wildly successful in his first game with Arizona, but he turned in a decent performance. James average less than 3 yards per carry and scored on a 1-yard run. Andre Johnson could have the best season of his young career with a decent number two receiver in Houston now. Andre caught 6 balls for 101 yards in the opener.

"He was running blind for a second there and then bounced into the line," Ho Train coach Jon Kraus said of his runningback Steven Jackson. "He made a cut and then sprinted in the for the score. It was a beautiful run."

Mike Bell wasn't the feature back in Denver this week (go figure), but he looked impressive with 5.8 yards per carry and a 1-yard touchdown. Owners will need to keep an eye on Bell's fractured finger. Anquan Boldin didn't get a lot of opportunities this week, catching just 4 balls for 62 yards. Boldin also scored on a 6-yard reception. Reggie Brown only caught a couple of passes this week, but he turned one of them into a 5-yard score.

"It would have been much worse if they had beaten us at our best," OakalDoakals coach Alan Hatok said. "But we didn't play well. It's like they say: 'On any given Sunday.'"
