(12/12/2006) — The Texas Nashvillians beat the Karpet Munchers 70-67 behind 22 points from RB Fred Taylor in a hotly contested matchup that went down to the wire.

Fred Taylor posted big numbers against Indy's weak run defense, rushing for 131 yards on just 9 carries and scoring on a 21-yard run. Terry Glenn was Romo's favorite target this week, resulting in 8 receptions for 150 yards, his first 100-yard game of the season. Larry Johnson may have had a quiet day, but it was good enough for him to take over the rushing lead. Johnson finished with 120 yards on the ground.

"We knew we had all the ingredients that it takes to play these guys well," Texas Nashvillians coach Randy Folks said. "Today we cooked up a victory."

T.J. Houshmandzadeh delivered another nice game this week with 8 catches for 118 yards and a 20-yard touchdown. Housh has scored in each of his last 3 games. Tony Romo completed 16 of 33 passes for 249 yards and a touchdown, but he had a difficult time putting together many meaningful drives in the first half.

"They put eight, nine in the box," Karpet Munchers coach John Keenan said. "Linebackers playing a little deeper to definitely take away the cutback run. It was a pretty good scheme, but you still had to make plays, just keep hammering away. We just got down and had to throw the ball more than we would like. Nothing was working well."

Pittsburgh (12/12/2006) — The Manic Moose offensive line dominated the trenches, opening up holes that LaDainian Tomlinson could have driven a truck through, but he decided to run while racking up 28 points.

Believe it or not, LaDainian Tomlinson had one of his quietest games of the season. LT rushed for 103 yards on 28 carries, but as usual, scored 3 more touchdowns to set the single season record. Willie Parker racked up the rushing yards this week with 223 yards on 32 carries, including a 3-yard score. Parker had a rushed for 129 yards in his previous three games combined. Antonio Gates had another big game on Sunday with over 100 yards and 2 scores. Gates now leads tight ends in touchdowns and yards.

"I felt like they could only contain us for so long, and we would find a way to make some plays," Manic Moose coach Matt Spinola said. "It speaks for how we stick together, how we made some plays in key situations."

Julius Jones surprised most owners this week by ripping off a 77-yard touchdown run early in the game. Jones finished with 116 yards, his first 100-yard game since week 6. Carson Palmer threw a season high 3 interceptions on Sunday, but he still managed to post some good fantasy numbers. Palmer scored twice and threw for 297 yards. Deuce McAllister didn't score this week, but he looked solid in the rushing game. McAllister rushed for 111 yards on 21 carries.

"These guys have been working their tails off," Carter's Commandos coach Chris Arenth said. "The veterans have been excellent. The effort has been good. Right now, we're just not putting together a complete game."

(12/12/2006) — Quarterbacks seem to get all the credit. This game was no different as Matt Leinart scored 20 points leading the Da Funk to a 96-70 victory over Tally Wackers.

Matt Leinart played a solid game this week with a couple of touchdowns and 232 yards on 21 of 34 passing. Leinart is still looking for his first 3-touchdown game of the season. As expected, Rudi Johnson got plenty of touches against Oakland this week. Rudi scored twice and rushed for 117 yards on 30 carries. Frank Gore bounced back from last week with 168 yards of total offense and a touchdown. Gore is now ranked 3rd in rushing yards and is 85 yards off the lead.

"I pay Mike Temme as little attention as I pay someone walking down the street," Mark Stahlsmith said. "They were the highly rated and now they have some losses. Maybe now we won't have to hear all their babble."

Eli Manning delivered his second solid game in a row with 3 touchdowns and 172 yards on 17 of 33 passing. Manning hadn't scored 3 times in a game since the third week of the season. Tatum Bell posted his second 100-yard rushing game in a row, but he is still having problems finding the end zone. Bell has just 2 touchdowns this season.

"I thought the game after the first half should have been close," Tally Wackers coach Mike Temme said. "If we could have just done a little something, we might have been able to get back in it. But the way we played, we really didn't have much chance."

Glenshaw (12/12/2006) — Winning is contagious. Just ask the Ho Train, who extended their winning streak to 7 games.

Steven Jackson posted over 100 yards of total offense for the eleventh time this season and scored 2 touchdowns, his first multi-score game of the season. Ladell Betts posted solid numbers for the third week in a row with 185 total yards. If Betts can get into the end zone more often he'll become a real fantasy favorite. Lee Evans proved his big play making ability again this week with a 77-yard touchdown reception. Four of Evans' five scores this season are 40 or more yards long.

"I think our coaching staff probably feels we'll get sharper as we go," Ho Train coach Jon Kraus said. "We kept telling people all along that we are right on schedule. We sure looked like it today."

Peyton Manning failed to throw a touchdown pass for the first time this season as Indy's struggles continued. Manning scored on a 1-yard run and threw for 313 yards. It looks like Torry Holt is getting his second wind just in time for the fantasy playoffs. Holt scored twice while grabbing 6 balls for 75 yards.

"It was a great game to coach until kickoff, and then it was a really hard game to coach," Otters coach Rich Raida said.
