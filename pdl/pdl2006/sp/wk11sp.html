Glenshaw (11/21/2006) — Whether you call them lucky or good, the Otters extended their consecutive win streak to 3 games with another good performance, this time at the expense of the Beers 63-42.

Joe Horn took part in New Orleans passing frenzy on Sunday with a 72-yard touchdown reception and 93 total receiving yards. Owners are still wondering where the Peyton Manning of old has been. Manning tossed a couple of touchdowns, was intercepted twice, and finished with 254 yards in Indy's first loss of the season. Wali Lundy split carries with Gado this week, but it suited him just fine. Lundy scored on a 17-yard run, rushed 8 times for 61 yards, and grabbed 4 balls for 22 receiving yards.

"Perseverance, character and never ever surrendering. I know for a fact that my guys are proud of how they came together today," said winning coach Rich Raida of the Otters.

Alex Smith played an outstanding game but he still struggles to post any big fantasy numbers. Smith scored twice and threw for 163 yards on 19 of 25 passing. Owners would love to see one running back in Dallas carry the load all by themselves, even for just one game. Marion Barber found the end zone twice this week, but got just 9 carries for 35 yards.

"No more excuses, we need results. We've had too many excuses this season. No member of this team has any job security, including the coaches," Beers coach Mike Spinola said.

(11/21/2006) — Displaying the physical skills that aren't seen very often, the high-powered Da Funk beat the Tally Wackers 76 - 29 in front of a national audience.

Matt Leinart had a nice outing this week with a short touchdown pass, a 9-yard touchdown run, and 233 yards in the air. Believe it or not, Frank Gore now has more rushing yards than Tomlinson this season. Gore had another outstanding game with 212 yards on the ground and 4 catches for 26 yards. Rudi Johnson has been asking for a more prominent role in Cincinnati's offense and he got his opportunity this week. Rudi rushed for 111 yards on 27 carries, his first 100-yard game since week 7.

"No one blinked an eye when we got behind," Da Funk coach Mark Stahlsmith said. "They just kept playing, and I'm especially proud of Matt Leinart scoring 23 points."

Deion Branch has definitely become one of Seattle's favorite targets. Branch scored a 38-yard touchdown and finished with 7 catches for 113 yards. Javon Walker was unable to keep his hot streak alive this week as he caught just 4 balls for 68 yards. Marc Bulger delivered a goose egg to owners this week with 142 yards on 19 of 34 passing and no scores. It was Bulger's second game in a row without a touchdown.

"I'd rather be wearing a bucket of Gatorade on the sidelines," Mike Temme said. "But I think I handled the situations very well. My team trusts I'll get the job done. I don't always do it, but I like the play calling in tough situations. I still have some tricks up my sleeve"

Tom Brady dominated this week with 4 touchdown passes and 244 yards. The way Brady was playing, it was unfortunate for owners that New England was winning by a wide margin. As usual, Larry Johnson quietly went about his business this week with 154 yards on the ground and a couple more touchdowns. Entering Monday's game, Johnson has a narrow lead in rushing yards. "A lot of times I'll put the pressure on myself," Texas Nashvillians coach Randy Folks said. "I have done that before here and it stirred people up, and I'll take the pressure. I know people have second-guessed me. I don't care. I make decisions based on what gives us the best opportunity to win. If it means I put my own head in the noose, so be it."
