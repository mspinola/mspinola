(10/10/2006) — Donovan McNabb might not get the attention he deserves but he sure received attention from those in attendance at the stadium after scoring 53 points in leading the F-Jackie to a come from behind win over the Carter's Commandos 81 - 33.

Donovan McNabb continued his domination of the quarterback position with 3 more touchdowns and over 350 yards in the air. McNabb is averaging over 300 yards per game and has 6 scores 30 yards or longer. Reggie Wayne finally scored, but it probably wasn't everything owners were hoping for. Wayne scored on a 2-yard catch and finished with just 3 receptions for 24 yards.

"Those guys are 0-and-whatever, but they're playing hard," said Tony Verdream, who made several good calls this week. "When a team plays hard, good things are going to happen, and it almost happened today. Another good player or two, and we're talking about how bad we are, and we're the laughingstock or whatever."

Plaxico Burress scored on a 2-yard reception and finished with 69 yards on a season high 7 receptions. If Burress can keep his head in the game he should get plenty of opportunities this season. Julius Jones delivered his second 100-yard game in a row, but his goal line scoring opportunities keep going to Marion Barber. If that changes Jones could post some decent fantasy numbers. Dominic Rhodes is slowly starting to post some acceptable numbers. Rhodes didn't score but he was able to post over 100 yards of offense with 84 rushing yards and 5 catches for 26 yards.

"I made questionable calls all day long. It was pathetic," Carter's Commandos coach Chris Arenth said. "We'll get it back on track next week."

Pittsburgh (10/10/2006) — Displaying the physical skills that aren't seen very often, the high-powered Tally Wackers beat the Manic Moose 81 - 58 in front of a national audience.

Greg Jennings looks more impressive every week. Sunday, Jennings scored on a 46-yard reception and finished with the second 100-yard game of his very young career. Eli Manning played a solid game on Sunday but his fantasy production left something to be desired. Manning's only score came on a 2-yard pass and he finished with 256 yards. Reggie Williams grabbed a 16-yard touchdown reception, his third score in the last 2 weeks. Williams looks to have a big game against Houston after his bye week.

"Perseverance, character and never ever surrendering. I know for a fact that my guys are proud of how they came together today," said winning coach Mike Temme of the Tally Wackers.

Antonio Gates came through this week with a 22-yard touchdown on a great catch. Gates is still struggling to get meaningful yardage; it was his fourth game in a row with less than 60 yards. Willie Parker did okay against a tough San Diego defense this week, rushing for 57 yards, grabbing 2 receptions for 26 yards and scoring on a 9-yard run. Bernard Berrian is having an unbelievable season. Berrian scored his fourth touchdown of the season and just missed another 100-yard game with 4 catches for 97 yards.

"We did have opportunities to win," coach Matt Spinola said. "I want my guys to be mad, I want my guys to be disappointed. But at the same time, be ready for next week."

Alex Smith bounced back from last week's disaster with an excellent game. Although Smith only threw for 165 yards, he completed 15 of 19 passes including 3 touchdowns. Maurice Jones-Drew is turning into a scoring machine with 4 touchdowns in his last 3 games. Sunday, Jones-Drew scored on 2 short runs and finished with 59 yards on 13 carries.
