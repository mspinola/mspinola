(11/7/2006) — Displaying the physical skills that aren't seen very often, the high-powered Texas Nashvillians beat the Beers 79 - 38 in front of a national audience.

Pittsburgh (11/7/2006) — Winning is contagious. Just ask the Manic Moose, who extended their winning streak to 4 games.

LaDainian Tomlinson had an average day this week (3 more touchdowns and 192 yards of total offense). After a relatively slow start to the season, Tomlinson is definitely on his way to a brilliant season. Willie Parker only had 14 rushing attempts on Sunday but that didn't stop him from having a great game. Parker had 137 yards of total offense and scored twice. Terrell Owens scored on a 4-yard reception and then went back to sleep. Owens wrapped up his day with 7 catches for 76 yards.

"It's kind of gutsy for where we were in the game. But those are the kind of things you've got to do. It definitely switched the momentum," Manic Moose coach Matt Spinola said of the offensive trickery employed late in the game.

Ahman Green produced another good game with over 120 yards rushing, his third 100-yard game in a row. Philip Rivers threw for over 200 yards but didn't have much success otherwise. Rivers had no turnovers this week but he had no scores either.

Glenshaw (11/7/2006) — The Otters beat the Iron City Pounders 78-62 behind 26 points from QB Peyton Manning in a brutal rivalry.

Peyton Manning didn't have a monster game this week, but he still managed to score on a couple of short passes and throw for over 300 yards. Marques Colston is on his way to rookie of the year honors if he keeps this up. Colson scored again this week and grabbed 11 balls for 123 yards. Warrick Dunn rushed for almost 5 yards per carry but he just didn't get enough opportunities this week. Dunn finished with 51 yards on the ground including a short touchdown.

"Was I into it today? What do you think?" said Otters coach Rich Raida, both arms caked in mud and grass stains. "I celebrated every fantasy point today".

Brett Favre threw a couple of interceptions, his first since week 4, and only had one touchdown on a 1-yard pass. Favre finished with 287 yards on 28 of 47 passing. Jeremy Shockey saw more action this week with Plaxico out of the lineup. Shockey scored on a 3-yard catch and finished with 8 receptions for 66 yards. Corey Dillon is still lacking in yardage, but at least he is still the go-to-guy at the goal line. Dillon scored on 2 short runs and finished with 48 yards rushing.

"It's a feeling we haven't had a for a while, Iron City Pounders coach Brian McIntyre said. "We're all a little shell-shocked right now. It's not that they're not a good team and didn't go out and beat us. But in a lot of respects, we beat ourselves."
