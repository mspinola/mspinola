(12/19/2006) — The Manic Moose and Da Funk entertained the crowds with play after play of highlight-film material.

LaDainian Tomlinson was spectacular again with 199 yards on the ground and two huge touchdown runs. Tomlinson now has a commanding 110 yard lead for the league's rushing title. Willie Parker followed up on last week's outstanding effort with his longest touchdown run of the season and 132 yards on the ground. Terrell Owens came up big at just the right time for owners with a couple of scores on 5 catches for 69 yards. Owens now leads wide receivers with 11 touchdowns.

Regarding the big game by LaDainian Tomlinson, Manic Moose coach Matt Spinola said, "That's a plus. No question about that. We just asked him to take pride in himself and prove to me he is a football player."

Frank Gore exploded in the fourth quarter of Thursday's game to give owners a big game. Gore scored on a 20-yard reception and finished with 178 yards of offense. Rudi Johnson got off to a great start on Monday night and then Cincinnati fell behind and he didn't get many more touches. Johnson scored on a 12 yard run and finished with just shy of 100 yards of total offense. Matt Leinart didn't have his best stuff this week as he completed 20 of 35 passes for 214 yards, no scores, and 2 interceptions.

"You mean my scheme?" Da Funk coach Mark Stahlsmith said, referring to the defense that allowed too many points this week. "I'm not going to get into specifics. It's the same scheme that has been successful in the past."

(12/19/2006) — The Ho Train made good on their opportunity to re-establish its "Commitment to Excellence."

Michael Vick had a nose for the end zone this week, converting 4 red zone opportunities into touchdown passes. Vick threw for 237 yards and added 56 yards on the ground. Steven Jackson delivered another solid performance this week with 127 yards on 31 carries and 2 touchdowns. Oakland prevented Jackson from recording a reception for the first time this season. Lee Evans continues to get the job done, this week with a 21-yard touchdown and 67 yards. Five of Evans' six scores have come in the second half of the season.

Smiling broadly and taking a puff from a fine cigar, Ho Train coach Jon Kraus said, "That's 8 wins in a row."

Tom Brady got the job done this week with a couple of scores, but his lack of yardage certainly didn't help owners. Brady threw for just 109 yards on 16 of 23 passing. Larry Johnson needs a couple of huge games if he has any chance at the rushing title. Johnson, who rushed for 84 yards on Sunday, is now 110 yards behind Tomlinson. Terry Glenn caught 5 balls for 96 yards but he failed to score for the third week in a row. Glenn has some favorable match ups the last 2 weeks of the regular season.

"It is just unfortunate what happened to us this week," Texas Nashvillians coach Randy Folks said. "But we're going to bounce back. For the teams that won, obviously it is a little more pressure on us to get it together since we're obviously favored each week. I look forward to reviewing stats this week and giving my team a chance."
