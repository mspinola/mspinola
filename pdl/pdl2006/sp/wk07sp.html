(10/24/2006) — The Texas Nashvillians, now 7-0, toyed with their fantasy league foe, building a large lead and posting the league's best point total this week, beating Beers 135-66.

Hines Ward was this week's stud performer, catching 8 passes for 171 yards and 3 touchdowns. Ward has caught more touchdown passes from Charlie Batch than Ben Roethlisberger this season. Larry Johnson is on a roll now. Johnson scored for the fourth game in a row and racked up over 160 yards of offense. Johnson's longest touchdown this season is 11 yards. Darrell Jackson scored a huge 72-yard touchdown on Sunday, but saw his productivity go down after Hasselbeck left due to an injury. Jackson finished with his second 100-yard game of the season.

"This was self-evaluation time," Texas Nashvillians coach Randy Folks said. "You have to look in the mirror. We had to have this one. The Beers was better than us on paper."

Randy Moss had his biggest game of the season with 129 yards on 7 receptions, including a 32-yard touchdown. Moss's woes may revisit him now if Andrew Walter is added to the injury list. Maurice Jones-Drew scored for the fourth game in a row, but he didn't have much luck in the rushing department. Jones-Drew rushed for 10 yards on 8 carries and added 58 yards on 7 catches. Andrew Walter tossed his best game of the season with over 250 yards, including a 32-yard touchdown. Walter has some tough match ups over the next few weeks.

"During all of our losses, somehow and someway, we've squandered away the lead," Beers coach Mike Spinola said.

(10/24/2006) — Alge Crumpler made sure that one of fantasy football's more exciting rivalries wasn't even close as the Da Funk (5-2) pounded the Blitzkrieg (2-5) 84-35.

Alge Crumpler made owners proud this week the best game of his life. Crumpler scored 3 touchdowns on 6 catches for 117 yards. Willis McGahee racked up more than 100 yards of offense for just the second time this season with 59 yards on the ground and 61 yards receiving. McGahee has scored just 1 touchdown. Rudi Johnson struggled early but kept plugging away in the second half where he was able to break free for some decent runs. Johnson finished with 101 yards on 26 carries.

"This is like taking 500-600 extra pounds on your back and just throwing it off, feeling like yourself again," Da Funk coach Mark Stahlsmith said. "This is huge for us. There's a feeling in here that we haven't felt in here for a long time. And really, it's about time. We've been beating up ourselves and playing some pretty undisciplined ball."

Contrary to some people's opinions, Donald Driver is still Brett Favre's favorite receiver. Driver pulled in 10 receptions for 93 yards and scored a 34-yard touchdown. Chad Pennington didn't have a huge game, but he looked good with 189 yards on 16 of 22 passing. Pennington also scored on a 44-yard hookup, his sixth score of 20 or more yards this season.

"Their coach has done a marvelous job there," Blitzkrieg coach Sean Berecek said. "Obviously. Wow. They have, I'm sure, exceeded even their own expectations, and they're very high there."

(10/24/2006) — Quarterbacks seem to get all the credit. This game was no different as Eli Manning scored 18 points leading the Tally Wackers to a 81-47 victory over Carter's Commandos.

Eli Manning scored on a 50-yard bomb in the opening drive, but that wasn't a good indicator of how the whole game would go. Manning completed just 12 passes for 189 yards and one interception. Tatum Bell delivered his best fantasy production of the season with a 9-yard touchdown run and 126 yards of offense. Javon Walker is making the most of being in a rush oriented offense. Walker nabbed 9 passes for 107 yards but has still only scored in one game this season.

"Things haven't been going their way. We knew that coming in. It was the same for us," Tally Wackers coach Mike Temme said. "You could say, `Yeah, it's just the Carter's Commandos.' But (other) teams look at us and say the same thing"

Carson Palmer still isn't the stud he was last season, but his game looks better every week. Palmer scored on a couple of passes and finished with 240 yards. Plaxico Burress grabbed a 50-yard jump ball to score on the opening drive and then grabbed just one more ball the rest of the game for 44 more yards.

"We did well enough to get into second- or third-and-short, but then we'd get a penalty," Carter's Commandos coach Chris Arenth said. "We started in a good situation and then put ourselves into a bad situation."
