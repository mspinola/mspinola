(11/14/2006) — The Da Funk home crowd cheered all day long in appreciation of the guts and determination displayed on the way to an important 74-32 win.

Frank Gore had a huge game going before he left in the third quarter with a concussion. Gore scored on a 61-yard run and finished with 173 yards of total offense. Matt Leinart is still heading in the wrong direction. Leinart threw for over 200 yards and ran in a score, but he completed only 20 of 38 passes and threw 2 interceptions. (11/14/2006) — Donald Driver might not get the attention he deserves but he sure received attention from those in attendance at the stadium after scoring 31 points in leading the Blitzkrieg to a come from behind win over the Scooter's Boozers 133 - 72.

Donald Driver had an outstanding day with 6 catches for 191 yards, including an 82-yard touchdown. Driver is now ranked second in receptions and fifth in yards. Drew Brees racked up 398 yards in the air on Sunday, but he had trouble getting the ball into the end zone. Brees' only score came on a short pass. Donte' Stallworth returned to the lineup with plenty of energy on Sunday, catching 6 balls for 139 yards and an 84-yard score.

"Well, it's another win," Blitzkrieg coach Sean Berecek said. "We have an opportunity. Whether we are good enough to take advantage of that I don't know."

Tiki Barber didn't let his injured thumb slow him down too much on Sunday. Barber, who maintained his rushing lead by adding 141 yards this week, is tied for 42nd in touchdowns by running backs this season. Jake Plummer threw for over 200 yards for the second game in a row and connected on a couple of touchdown passes. Plummer was also intercepted 3 times on Sunday. Reggie Bush scored the first rushing touchdown of his career on a 15-yard run and finished with 89 offensive yards. Bush now has 54 receptions this season, tops among running backs.

"They came to play today," Scooter's Boozers coach Scott Arrigo said. "And they made a number of big plays. They did the job that they had to do. We got behind the 8-ball in the first half and couldn’t overcome it."

(11/14/2006) — Focused more on winning than avoiding mistakes , the F-Jackie (6-4) turned in several big plays in an easy win over the Beers (3-7).

Donovan McNabb didn't have a huge game this week, but he always seems to find a way to deliver a huge play. McNabb scored on an 84-yard touchdown pass and finished with 220 yards passing. Brian Westbrook was held scoreless for the second game in a row, but he still racked up some significant yardage. Westbrook rushed for 113 yards and caught 3 balls for 37 yards. Reggie Wayne grabbed a 1-yard pass for his only score of the game and finished with just 4 catches for 42 yards. Wayne is way overdue for a huge game.

"We didn't panic when a couple guys went down. This isn't semipro," said F-Jackie head coach Buddy Verdream. "Guys are paid to coach fantasy players. I knew the win today meant just as much to all of them as it does to me."

Marion Barber had his biggest workload of the season with 14 carries and he delivered a season high 65 rushing yards and a touchdown. Maurice Jones-Drew scored his sixth touchdown of the season but he didn't get enough opportunities to rack up any good yardage. Jones-Drew finished with 67 yards of total offense.
