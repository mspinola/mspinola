(9/19/2006) — RB Rudi Johnson scored 26 points to help the Da Funk beat OakalDoakals 79-24.

Rudi Johnson racked up 145 yards on the ground and scored on touchdown runs from 1 and 9 yards. Entering Monday's game, Rudi trails just Warrick Dunn in rushing yards. Some people are still asking if Frank Gore is for real, and two weeks into the season it still looks like it. Gore scored on a 32-yard run and finished with 127 yard rushing. Matt Hasselbeck started red hot, scoring on a 49-yard touchdown and throwing for over 100 yards in the first quarter. Hasselbeck was held scoreless the rest of the way and finished with 221 yards.

"The first quarter was the worst quarter of football from my perspective I've ever been associated with as a coach," Da Funk coach Mark Stahlsmith said. "I was very pleased with the way the game turned out, but I wasn't very happy with the way it started. We got better during the course of the game. It's the biggest swing I've ever been around."

Reggie Brown scored on his only reception of the game, a 23-yarder. Brown has only caught 3 balls this season, two of them for touchdowns.

"I was happy to get to the stadium. We had a turbulent flight, hotel alarms rang all night, the food stunk, and the bus broke down heading to the stadium, said Alan Hatok, losing coach of the OakalDoakals. "I would have liked it to have turned out in our favor but that's life."

Pittsburgh (9/19/2006) — Focused more on winning than avoiding mistakes , the Carter's Commandos (1-1) turned in several big plays in an easy win over the Manic Moose (0-2).

Carson Palmer struck early with 2 touchdowns in the first quarter, but he couldn't find the end zone after that. Palmer threw for over 350 yards but he was intercepted twice and sacked 4 times. Antonio Bryant hasn't caught many balls this season, but he has proven himself to be a big play maker. Bryant scored on 72-yard play and is averaging over 30 yards per reception on the season. Laveranues Coles scored on a 46-yard reception and pulled in 6 balls for his second 100-yard game of the season. Coles is definitely benefiting from Pennington's improved play.

"Turnovers have been the difference for us the last few weeks," Carter's Commandos coach Chris Arenth said. "If you can't get in the end zone, at least put the offense in a situation where it can capitalize."

LaDainian Tomlinson's only problem this week was that the game was out of reach so early. Before taking a rest, LT scored 2 touchdowns and racked up 122 yards of offense. Daunte Culpepper was held in check the entire game until he connected on a 23-yard touchdown pass in the final two minutes. Culpepper completed 23 of 32 passes for 250 yards. So far, Antonio Gates isn't getting as many looks as last season. This week Gates caught 4 passes for 55 yards.

"I'm not going to sit here feeling sorry for myself," said losing coach Matt Spinola. "The Carter's Commandos beat us today, but I definitely don't need a box of tissues, because I'm fine."
