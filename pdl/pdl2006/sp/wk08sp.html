Glenshaw (10/31/2006) — Quarterbacks seem to get all the credit. This game was no different as Tom Brady scored 46 points leading the Texas Nashvillians to a 157-118 victory over Otters.

Tom Brady had a big night on Monday with 4 touchdowns and over 350 yard passing. It was by far Brady's best game this year, setting his highs in scores, yards, and passer rating this season. Larry Johnson had a huge day, but he earned it by carrying the ball 39 times. Johnson scored 4 times, rushed for 155 yards, and grabbed 2 passes for 26 yards. Darrell Jackson didn't get many balls thrown his way this week but he sure knew what to do with one of them (49 yard TD).

"Sometimes, it's just one of those things," Texas Nashvillians coach Randy Folks said. "We match up very well against the Otters."

Peyton Manning was nearly flawless this week as he completed 32 of 39 passes for 345 yards and 3 touchdowns. Manning has 7 touchdowns and 687 yards in his past 2 games. Joe Horn had a big game with a 32-yard touchdown reception and 126 yards. Horn's last 2 games have been his best of the season (both with a score and over 100 yards). Chris Henry came back from his suspension ready to play. Henry caught 5 balls for 81 yards, including a huge 55-yard touchdown.

"They put eight, nine in the box," Otters coach Rich Raida said. "Linebackers playing a little deeper to definitely take away the cutback run. It was a pretty good scheme, but you still had to make plays, just keep hammering away. We just got down and had to throw the ball more than we would like. Nothing was working well."

Pittsburgh (10/31/2006) — RB LaDainian Tomlinson scored 56 points to help the Manic Moose extend their win streak, topping the Dunbar Dogs 100-63.

LaDainian Tomlinson was amazing again this week. LT racked up 240 yards of total offense and scored 3 touchdowns, giving him 9 touchdowns in his last 3 games. Willie Parker scored his longest touchdown of the season on a 25-yard reception and finished with 111 yards of total offense. Parker is tied for fourth in touchdowns by running backs. Terrell Owens got a lot of looks from Tony Romo this week, turning them into 9 receptions for 107 yards. Surprisingly, it was Owens' first 100-yard game of the season.

"Today was a big step for us because we came out and made plays for the first time in a while," Manic Moose coach Matt Spinola said.

Ben Watson turned in his best game of the season with 7 catches for 95 yards and a touchdown. Michael Jenkins benefited from Michael Vick's newfound passing skills by scoring on a 26-yard reception. Jenkins finished with 3 receptions for 53 yards. Ben Roethlisberger racked up over 300 yards passing and a touchdown, but his 4 interceptions and 5 sacks made for a miserable day.

"Our main problems executing this week were turnovers and a lackadaisical effort on the offense," Dunbar Dogs coach Erik Farmerie said. "It's my job to make sure that we rise to the occasion next week."
