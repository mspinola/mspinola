Pittsburgh (12/26/2006) — Party time continued for the streaking Ho Train as they beat the Manic Moose to improve their record to 12-4 with 9 wins in a row.

Steven Jackson had a monster performance this week with 150 yards rushing, 6 catches for 102 yards receiving and 2 long scores. Jackson trails by 95 yards in total yardage by running backs. Ladell Betts continued his impressive streak with 164 yards of total offense and 2 scores. Betts now has over 1000 yards rushing on the season. Lee Evans grabbed 5 balls for 64 yards, including a 37-yard score. Evans has scored in each of his last 3 games and has 7 scores on the season.

"We were very fortunate to come in here and find a way to win," Ho Train coach Jon Kraus said. "We played an excellent football team. I felt that they played extremely hard, and we made some plays when we had to make them. I feel really good about our football team right now because we went on the road and found a way to win some games in a row."

Plaxico Burress started the game with a great play, a 55-yard touchdown reception, but didn't catch another ball the rest of the game. LaDainian Tomlinson's scoring streak came to an end this week, but he still made owners proud with 123 yards on 22 rushing attempts. Willie Parker had a rough day against Baltimore's defense this week, gaining just 29 yards on 13 carries.

"Yes, having great fantasy software should have improved my ability to set the Manic Moose lineup,” said Matt Spinola, who was clearly distraught after the loss. "But no excuses. We didn't play well. It wasn’t just the lineup that caused our loss this week."
