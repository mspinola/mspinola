(11/2/2004)  Displaying the physical skills that aren't seen very often, the high-powered Beers beat the Iron City Pounders 80 - 61 in front of a national audience.

Vinny Testaverde leaned on his tight end and completed 19 of 24 passes for 235 yards and 3 passing touchdowns, he also ran in a score from 3 yards out. Rod Gardner is officially a red zone threat. He snagged 3 balls for 41 yards including a pair of 12-yard scores. No other wide receiver on his team has scored a touchdown this year. Playing from behind, Reuben Droughns did not receive the carries he is accustomed to. He had 15 attempts for 49 yards and caught 6 passes for 63 yards.

"You can think about how many fantasy points each of your players have accumulated after the season," said Beers coach Mike Spinola. "But right now the Ws are a lot more important."

Curtis Martin bounced back from last week's disappointment with a strong performance, rushing for 115 yards and a 25-yard touchdown. It was Martin's fifth 100-yard game of the season. There has been a worm sighting. No, not Dennis Rodman but the break dancing of Johnnie Morton. He had 5 catches for 68 yards and performed the dance after his 7-yard score.

"A lot of times I'll put the pressure on myself," Beers coach Mike Spinola said. "I have done that before here and it stirred people up, and I'll take the pressure. I know people have second-guessed me. I don't care. I make decisions based on what gives us the best opportunity to win. If it means I put my own head in the noose, so be it."
