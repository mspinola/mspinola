(11/23/2004)  Coach Scott Arrigo knew that John Keenan was going to use a power running game this week but he still couldn't prevent Brian Westbrook from running like a freight train. Brian Westbrook scored 35 points for the Karpet Munchers as they dominated the line of scrimmage, beating the Scooter's Boozers 97-59.

Brian Westbrook picked up 63 rushing yards on 12 carries and 42 receiving yards on 5 catches. Westbrook caught 2 touchdown passes from 1 and 14 yards out. Brett Favre attempted 50 passes in a game for the first time in 4 years, the 5th time in his career with 50 or more passes. He completed 33 passes for 383 yards, 1 touchdown and 2 interceptions. Marvin Harrison grabbed 4 passes for 49 yards including a 10-yard strike, his first score in the past 3 weeks. Harrison's production has faded with the emergence of other receivers.

I really shouldnt tell you why we were successful, said Karpet Munchers coach John Keenan. But I will tell you that any time you're going against a coach like Scott Arrigo, you want to keep the coach confused and off-balance. I dont know if balance was an issue but I think confusion really set in. "

Kerry Collins had his best outing of 2004. Collins completed 18 of 30 passes for 227 yards with 2 touchdowns and no turnovers. It was his first game this year that he did not throw an interception. Jerome Bettis picked up 129 yards on 29 carries, but the "Bus" didn't make a stop in the end zone this week. He has gained over 100 yards in each of his last 3 games. Tiki Barber failed to reach the endzone for only the 2nd time this season. But Barber broke the 100-yard mark for the 3rd time in 4 games with 107 yards on 21 carries plus 24 yards on 3 catches.

"We were able to come up with a few turnovers, but we weren't able to turn them into points," Scooter's Boozers coach Scott Arrigo said. "That's the big difference."
