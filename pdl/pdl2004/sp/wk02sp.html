(9/21/2004)  Displaying the physical skills that aren't seen very often, the high-powered Otters beat the Bunchalosers 83 - 61 in front of a national audience.

Peyton Manning played a solid game as he threw 2 touchdown passes on 24 of 33 passing. Manning finished with his second 250-yard game of the season. Torry Holt appears to be back on track, catching 9 passes for 121 yards and a 33-yard touchdown. Warrick Dunn's yardage hasn't been very impessive thus far (54 yards rushing per game), but he has turned into a scoring machine. Dunn scored his 3rd and 4th touchdowns of the season on touchdown runs.

"It was one of those days where things went right," Otters coach Rich Raida said.

Clinton Portis didn't have a breakout game on the ground this week, but he scored on a 13-yard pass reception. Portis finished with 98 yards of total offense. Phil Dawson racked up the kicking points this week as he booted 4 field goals. Daniel Graham only caught 2 passes for 21 yards, but both of them were for touchdowns. Graham leads tight ends with 3 touchdowns.

"Well, the Otters came to play," Bunchalosers coach Phil Arrigo said. "They made a number of big plays, did the job they had to do."

Pittsburgh (9/21/2004)  QB Daunte Culpepper scored 19 points to help the Manic Moose beat Carter's Cammandos 81-47.

Daunte Culpepper would have had another big game if he didn't lose a fumble at the goal line and another touchdown run wasn't called back. But Culpepper still finished strong with almost 350 yards passing and a touchdown pass. LaDanian Tomlinson rebounded nicely from last week's negative receiving yardage with 3 catches for 76 yards. Tomlinson also rushed for 87 yards and scored on a 4-yard run. Terrell Owens scored on a 45-yard touchdown reception and finished with 79 yards. Owens leads receivers with 4 touchdowns this season.

Despite the satisfying victory, the Manic Moose weren't boastful after the game. "It feels good, but everyone misinterpreted my "victory guarantee" thing," Matt Spinola said. "I didn't mean any disrespect to the Carter's Cammandos. What I wanted to do was add fuel to our fire, make a statement."

Corey Dillon was a work horse this week as he carried the ball 32 times for 158 yards. Dillon hasn't scored yet this season, but if he keeps getting the touches he could make many fantasy owners very happy. Laveranues Coles turned in his first 100-yard game of the season. Unfortunately, Coles also took a 16-yard loss on his only rushing attempt. Duce Staley is still waiting for his first touchdown of the season. Staley rushed 16 times for just 57 yards this week.

"We didn't score enough," Carter's Cammandos coach Chris Arenth said. "You have to take advantage of the opportunities presented to you. It's frustrating."

(9/21/2004)  In a typical Buddy Verdream-led performance, the F-Jackie overcame penalties and turnovers to out play the Tally Wackers 85-78.

Donovan McNabb is the hottest QB in the league right now. McNabb threw 3 more touchdown passes and finished with 245 yards in the air. Reggie Wayne was the go-to guy this week, catching 7 passes for 119 yards. Wayne also scored his first touchdown of the season. Last season Isaac Bruce had just 2 100-yard games. Bruce has already matched that feat this season as he pulled in 8 catches for 102 yards.

"We knew good things were eventually going to happen and today we made plays down the stretch and good things happened for our ball club," smiling coach Buddy Verdream said.

DeShaun Foster didn't disappoint owners in his first start of the season. Foster was unstoppable, rushing for a career high 174 yards and 1 touchdown. Javon Walker set a career high with 7 receptions and finished with 102 yards. It was Walker's second 100-yard game of his career.

(9/21/2004)  Scott Arrigo, despite making several good lineup decisions, was ultimately out coached by Brian McIntyre of the Iron City Pounders 50 - 45.

Curtis Martin followed up last week's dominating performance with another outstanding performance. Martin rushed for 119 yards and scored on 2 touchdown runs. Santana Moss is still waiting for his first touchdown of the season. Moss is averaging just 4 catches per game, but he almost got his first 100-yard game of the season.

Charlie Garner didn't have strong fantasy numbers this week, but he had a productive day on the ground. Garner averaged 5.8 yards per carry and finished with 75 yards rushing.
