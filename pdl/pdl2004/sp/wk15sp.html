(12/21/2004) — Friendships were quickly put aside in the week that led up to this game as Otters coach Rich Raida scripted a series of trick of plays that helped them beat the Karpet Munchers 92-70.

Drew Bennett has been on fire with 8 touchdown receptions in his last 3 games. This week he caught 13 passes for 160 yards and 2 touchdown receptions from 17 and 23 yards out. Warrick Dunn accumulated 134 yards on 28 carries and scored on a 6-yard run. He posted his first back-to-back 100-yard rushing games this season. Peyton Manning snapped his streak of throwing for 2 or more scores in each game this season. This week he completed 20 of 33 passes for 249 yards and a 29-yard score.

"We knew we had all the ingredients that it takes to play these guys well," Otters coach Rich Raida said. "Today we cooked up a victory."

Brett Favre completed 30 of 44 pass attempts for 367 yards and 2 scores but he also threw 3 interceptions. In his last 3 games he has 3 scores and 5 interceptions. Marvin Harrison pulled in 5 passes for 73 yards and scored on a wide open 29-yard reception. He has 14 touchdowns on the season with 7 coming in his last 5 games. Brian Westbrook failed to reach the end zone for the second game in a row. Westbrook rushed 12 times for 48 yards and caught 8 passes for 78 yards.

"We can't always rely on us coming back at the end," Karpet Munchers coach John Keenan said.

(12/21/2004) — It looked like something out of the Twilight Zone as the F-Jackie squandered a late lead to go home empty handed, losing 110-51.

Fred Taylor broke off several big runs as he averaged 7.5 yards per carry and scored for the 3rd time this season on a 37-yard run. He racked up 165 yards on 22 carries. Roy Williams benefited from a more aggressive passing attack this week as he caught 7 passes for 104 yards and scored on passes from 1 and 9 yards. Byron Leftwich was pressured all day and took several vicious hits as he released the ball, but he avoided turning it over. He completed 9 of 20 passes for 121 yards and 2 scores.

"I don't think people expected us to come out and play this well," said Da Funk coach Mark Stahlsmith. "But a lot of guys were wanting to play, just to play."

Muhsin Muhammad has scored in his last 3 games and in 5 of his last 6 games. He caught 10 passes for the second time in 3 weeks and turned them into 135 yards with a 6-yard score. Sammy Morris punched in 2 short touchdowns and finished with over 70 yards of total offense. It was the first multi-touchdown game of the season for Morris. Donovan McNabb has scored at least once in each of his last 6 games. This week he completed 20 of 35 pass attempts for 223 yards with a 2-yard score and 2 interceptions.

"I'm disappointed for our city and fans," F-Jackie coach Tony Verdream said. "The stands were rockin’ and the fans were behind us 100%, and we appreciate the support. All I can say is, we'll be back."
