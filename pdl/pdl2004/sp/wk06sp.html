Pittsburgh (10/19/2004) — Quarterbacks seem to get all the credit. This game was no different as Daunte Culpepper scored 60 points leading the Manic Moose to a 144-104 victory over Beers.

Daunte Culpepper set the single season record for 5-touchdown games with his third huge game of the season. And if that wasn't enough, Culpepper also threw for 425 yards. Last week Deuce McAllister returned early from an ankle injury to rush for 102 yards. This week McAllister's yardage number were down (78 yards on 18 carries), but he scored on 2 short touchdown runs. Brandon Lloyd's fantasy production continues to improve as he comes back from his injury. This week Lloyd caught 6 passes for 93 yards and a 33-yard touchdown.

"We could probably run our routes with our eyes closed, the way our passing game is clicking," said Manic Moose coach Matt Spinola.

Reuben Droughns carried the load again as he rushed 38 times for 176 yards and a 4-yard touchdown. In his 2 starts, Droughns is averaging 34 rushing attempts for 184.5 yards. Aaron Brooks has gotten plenty of throwing opportunities this season, but he isn't finding the endzone very often. Brooks completed 22 of 38 passes for 249 yards and 1 touchdown. He also scored his first rushing touchdown of the season. Randy Moss caught his league leading 8th touchdown pass on a 43-yard bomb. Moss left the game early with a leg injury, but he managed to finish with 89 yards receiving.

"Nobody has surrendered yet," Beers coach Mike Spinola said. "We're trying to keep the pride. It's just getting hard right now."
