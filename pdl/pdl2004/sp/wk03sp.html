Pittsburgh (9/28/2004) — Whether you call them lucky or good, the Manic Moose extended their consecutive win streak to 3 games with another good performance, this time at the expense of the Blitzkrieg 118-65.

Daunte Culpepper threw for over 300 yards for the second week in a row, connected on 2 short touchdown passes, and scored on a 1-yard touchdown run. Culpepper leads in passing yards and has the highest rating in the league. Aaron Stecker made the most of his first start of the season with a career-high 106 yards on the ground including a 42-yard touchdown. Terrell Owens scored his 5th touchdown of the season and turned in his first 100-yard game of the season. Owens is tied for the league lead in touchdowns by wide receivers.

"You can grow from slow starts and having to overcome that," Manic Moose coach Matt Spinola said. "This team grew today."

Shaun Alexander only had 74 yards of total offense, but he found the endzone 3 times for the second time season. Alexander didn't score more than 2 touchdowns in a game all last season. Donald Driver found the endzone on a 27-yard touchdown reception and finished with 64 yards. Driver's receptions and yards have improved each week this season.

"This goes on my shoulders," Blitzkrieg coach Sean Berecek said. "This is my fault, and I take the full responsibility for it. I need to do a better job of being more aggressive in the second half and putting the players in a better position, and that is what I will do."

(9/28/2004) — The Da Funk were back to their old tricks -- a field goal snafu, special team shenanigans, and an offense that was humbled early and often as they lost to F-Jackie 91-91.

Donovan McNabb continued to impress fantasy owners with his third consistent performance of the season. McNabb passed for over 350 yards and scored 3 touchdowns, his third game in a row with 3 or more touchdowns. Reggie Wayne pulled in 11 catches for 184 yards, including a 36-yard touchdown. In his last 2 games, Wayne has caught 18 passes for 303 yards. Isaac Bruce ran away with over 100 yards receiving for the 3rd game in a row as he caught 8 passes for 134 yards. Bruce now leads the league in receptions and receiving yards.

"You can think about how many fantasy points each of your players have accumulated after the season," said F-Jackie coach Tony Verdream. "But right now the Ws are a lot more important."

Roy Williams is making his rookie season a memorable one. For the second consecutive week Williams caught 2 touchdown passes, but this week he also got his first 100-yard game of his career. Fred Taylor scored his first touchdown of the season on a 1-yard run. Taylor, who finished with 81 yards on the ground, also caught 1 pass for 11 yards.

Lewis averaged over 10 yards per carry and finished the day with over 230 yards of total offense. Marvin Harrison didn't see too many touches this week, but he found a way to score on a 28-yard reception. Harrison is still waiting for his first 100-yard game of the season. "We didn't play as well as we have been defensively, but we made the plays when we had to," Karpet Munchers coach John Keenan said. "That's what good teams do."

Eddie George struggled on the ground as he rushed 11 times for just 19 yards, an average of 1.7 yards per carry. George scored his second touchdown of the season on a 1-yard run.
