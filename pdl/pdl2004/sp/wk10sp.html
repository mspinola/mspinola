(11/16/2004)  Buddy Verdream came into the game with an aggressive plan and the F-Jackie executed to perfection as they beat the lifeless Beers 105-69.

Donovan McNabb had an incredible first half with over 250 yards in the air and 3 touchdowns. McNabb finished the game with over 345 yards and 4 touchdowns. Ahman Green was held scoreless for the first time in 4 games. Green was able to slice through the defense for 145 yards on 21 carries and added 7 receiving yards on 2 catches. Isaac Bruce caught 7 passes and broke the 100-yard mark (104) for the first time in 5 games. A head injury to Torry Holt made it important that Bruce had a good game.

"Why did we win? Because I had Mike Spinola off-balance. Believe me, it's not because we are bigger and stronger than they are," F-Jackie coach Buddy Verdream said.

Aaron Brooks hooked up 15 times for 259 yards, including a 42-yard bomb to Joe Horn. Four of Brooks five interceptions this season have come in the past 5 games. Emmitt Smith rushed 19 times for 67 yards and a pair of touchdowns as the veteran showed he still has a nose for the endzone. He has scored in 7 of his 9 games this year.

The great coaching experiment for the Beers endured another harsh blow, certainly leading to more fan hate mail. "I'm running out of excuses," said Mike Spinola. "We need to catch a break."

Pittsburgh (11/16/2004)  After hours of film study, the Manic Moose crafted a plan that handled everything the Texas Nashvillians could throw at them, winning 221-64 and improving their record to 9-1.

Daunte Culpepper found his rhythm as he connected on 27 of 44 passes for 363 yards and 4 touchdowns, his first multiple touchdown game in 4 weeks. Terrell Owens turned in big plays all day long as he extended his league lead in touchdown receptions with 3 more this week. Owens wrapped up his day with his 6th 100-yard game of the season. Joe Horn came away with 5 passes for 167 yards and a 42-yard score capped off by giving the goal post a big hug! He really missed the endzone, he had not been there in 2 games.

Asked about the big win, coach Matt Spinola said, "I anticipated it. This game was on my mind for a long time. I hate those guys"

Jimmy Smith scored his third touchdown of the season, all of them from 25 yards out or longer. He had 109 yards including the 36-yard touchdown. Tom Brady has thrown 2 touchdown passes in each of the last 3 games. On Sunday he completed 19 of 35 passes for 233 yards and 2 red zone touchdowns. Marshall Faulk carried the ball 18 times for 139 yards and had 5 receptions for 21 yards. He was held out of the endzone for the third week in a row and only has 3 touchdowns on the year.

"I don't know what the difference between the first and second half was today," Texas Nashvillians coach Randy Folks said. "I guess we got a little complacent and didn't get our best players on the field this week."
