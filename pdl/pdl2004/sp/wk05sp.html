Pittsburgh (10/12/2004) — Throwing darts for most of the game, Daunte Culpepper scored 69 points, helping the Manic Moose beat the Tally Wackers 157 - 102.

Daunte Culpepper continued to have a hot hand as he threw for 300 yards for the third game in a row. Culpepper, who scored 5 touchdowns for the second time this season, is tied for the league lead with 14. Antonio Gates tied his career high of 8 receptions as he racked up 93 yards and two touchdowns, his first game with multiple touchdowns. LaDainian Tomlinson had a hard time finding running room as he rushed for only 56 yards on 19 carries, including a 1-yard touchdown. He was more active in the passing game as he gained 78 yards receiving on 4 catches.

"I'm all right," the Manic Moose coach Matt Spinola said. "Now, tomorrow I might be sore from all of the high-fives. My adrenaline's still flowing, but right now I feel all right. ... Today, I was just in a zone."

The halftime pep talk did the trick as Marc Bulger came out throwing for 273 yards (325 total) on 16 of 28 passing in the second half, including a 52-yard bomb at the end of the game. Bulger was also picked off 3 times in the game. Javon Walker's outstanding season continued this week with 8 catches for 159 yards and a 1-yard touchdown. Walker leads the league in receiving yards. Darrell Jackson has caught at least 5 passes in each game this season, but he has yet to deliver a 100-yard game. This week Jackson caught 5 passes for 91 yards including a 56-yard touchdown.

"We’ll challenge them," Tally Wackers coach Mike Temme said. "They’ll find something to moan about, but we’ll challenge the receivers at the same time being smart about it. We’re going to try to be aggressive. If the offense makes a play, so be it. We’re going to get our share of plays, too. Today they made the plays."

(10/12/2004) — When the going got tough Da Funk coach Mark Stahlsmith got tougher, leading the team to 74-70 victory over the Dunbar Dogs.

Matt Hasselbeck was very efficient this week as he completed 20 of 35 passes for 216 yards, 2 TDs and for the second game in a row he didn't turn the ball over. In 4 games this season he has thrown 6 touchdowns and only 2 interceptions. Eric Johnson had a monster game as he caught 13 passes for 162 yards and a 6-yard touchdown. He has caught at least 5 passes in each game this season and 10 or more passes for 100 or more yards in the past two games. Derrick Mason found the endzone for the second week in a row, but he finished with just 63 yards receiving. Mason has had just one 100-yard game this season.

"Enough talk about this team being soft," said Da Funk coach Mark Stahlsmith. "We showed them that we can gut out a big win."

Ben Roethlisberger has stepped into the starter role quite nicely. He has 6 touchdowns (5 passing) in 3 starts and 1 relief appearance. Roethlisberger threw a 37-yard touchdown and also scored on a diving 6-yard TD run. Keary Colbert had his best day as a rookie. He turned 4 catches into 115 yards and a 26-yard touchdown. In three starts, Colbert has caught 2 TD passes and is averaging 18.3 yards per reception. Michael Clayton's rookie season has been better than expected. He ended Sunday's game with 4 catches for 61 yards and 2 rushes for 24 yards. Through 5 weeks he has 23 receptions, 301 receiving yards and 1 TD.

"We fell short and that falls with me," Dunbar Dogs coach Erik Farmerie said. "We did not win and that is my responsibility. That is how you are judged in this business and so I cannot feel anything different than the fact that we threw our heart and soul into this thing and we had high expectations and we did not meet them. So, there is a feeling that it was a job undone."

David Carr found himself in a shootout on Sunday afternoon. Carr stepped up and threw for 372 yards on 27 of 43 passing and no interceptions as he capped off 3 drives with touchdown passes. Ashley Lelie only caught 2 balls on Sunday, but he took one of them 39 yards for a touchdown. It was his second touchdown of the season, each of them over 30 yards. In week 5 he continued his unblemished season by kicking 3 more FGs from 24, 34 and 46 yards.

Jeremy Shockey has caught 5 passes in each of the past 4 games, has gained at least 36 yards in all 5 games this season and has caught a touchdown pass in the past 2 games. He finished with 5 receptions for 44 yards.

"I don't see us falling apart," Iron City Pounders coach Brian McIntyre said. "We will be a playoff team. That's what it's all about. You've got to put a little fire under you in this situation, and that's in me."
