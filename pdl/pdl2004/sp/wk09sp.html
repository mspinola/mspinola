(11/9/2004)  "Hines Ward made a great move at the line, but I thought we were using an extra player or something," Texas Nashvillians coach Randy Folks said. "I didn't think he could get that wide open."

Hines Ward broke out of his scoring slump this week with a 16-yard run and a 20-yard touchdown reception. Ward hadn't scored since the third week of the season. Tom Brady threw for 234 yards on 18 of 31 passing attempts and scored on 2 touchdown passes from 2 and 4 yards out. Brady has scored 12 of his 15 touchdowns from inside the 20-yard line. Marshall Faulk picked up 66 yards on 12 carries and caught 6 passes for 22 yards. Faulk ran in a 2-point conversion but did not score a touchdown for the second game in a row.

"The week was incredible," Texas Nashvillians coach Randy Folks said. "People were talking bad about us, but we came together as a team."

Reuben Droughns carried the load with 29 carries for 120 yards and caught 1 pass for 6 yards. Droughns continues to rack up the yards but he has not scored in 3 games. Emmitt Smith failed to average 3 yards per carry for the second game in a row. Smith finished with 42 yards on 19 carries including a 5-yard touchdown run. Aaron Brooks completed 16 of 29 attempts for 173 yards with a 6-yard touchdown and 1 interception. Brooks has 10 scores on the season and only 1 of them has been longer than 17 yards.

"After Hines Ward broke it open with the catch and run, it jump-started everything," Texas Nashvillians coach Randy Folks said. "It took the pressure off the defense to some extent, because we were faltering miserably in the first half."
