(12/14/2004) — Friendships were quickly put aside in the week that led up to this game as Karpet Munchers coach John Keenan scripted a series of trick of plays that helped them beat the Beers 83-69.

T.J. Houshmandzadeh showed real courage continually running patterns over the middle and paying the price. He caught a career high 12 balls for 145 yards. Onterrio Smith scored his first touchdown in 4 weeks on a 5-yard run, his third score of the season. Smith gained 56 yards on 14 carries and caught 4 passes for 41 yards. Brian Westbrook picked up 59 yards on 12 carries and 59 more yards on 6 receptions. He has broken the 100-yard mark for combined offensive yards in each of his last 5 games.

"We're going to learn as a team it's not what you do, it's when you do it," Karpet Munchers coach John Keenan said.

Joey Galloway scored on a 36-yard reception for the second week in a row. He caught 5 balls for 76 yards and 2 touchdowns, the second being a 4-yard touchdown strike. Nick Goings went over the 100-yard mark for the fourth consecutive game and has scored 5 times over those four games. He picked up 108 yards on 31 carries. Aaron Brooks completed 18 of 31 pass attempts for 252 yards with a 31-yard score and 2 interceptions. He has thrown 2 or more picks in each of his last 4 games.

"Embarrassing is one thought that comes to mind," Beers coach Mike Spinola said. "It seemed like we went out and warmed up and turned around and it was over. I'll have to watch film. It's tough to swallow right now."

Pittsburgh (12/14/2004) — Quarterbacks seem to get all the credit. This game was no different as Matt Hasselbeck scored 39 points leading the Da Funk to a 110-90 victory over Manic Moose.

Matt Hasselbeck completed 23 of 34 passes for 334 yards with 3 scores and 2 picks. He has scored one-third (6) of his 18 touchdowns this season in the past 2 weeks. Willis McGahee has broken the 100-yard mark in 6 of his last 9 games. This week he carried the ball 27 times for 105 yards and 2 scores. All 9 of his scores have been on the ground. Derrick Mason didn't see too many opportunities as he caught just 3 passes for 22 yards. Mason scored on a 4-yard pass in the final minutes of the game.

"Me and Matt Spinola have been going at it all season," Da Funk coach Mark Stahlsmith said. "What I did this week wasn't dirty. It was just stuff you do in fantasy football."

Eddie Kennison made the most of his opportunities this week as he turned both of his receptions into touchdowns. All 6 of Kennison's touchdowns have come in the last 5 games. Deuce McAllister carried the ball 30 times for the first time this season. He could only muster 83 yards rushing but punched it in for 2 scores. He added 37 more yards on 4 receptions. LaDainian Tomlinson racked up 131 yards and punched in a 7-yard touchdown on 25 carries. He also caught 4 passes for 19 yards. He has scored 6 times in his last 4 games.

"It's a feeling we haven't had a for a while, Manic Moose coach Matt Spinola said. "We're all a little shell-shocked right now. It's not that they're not a good team and didn't go out and beat us. But in a lot of respects, we beat ourselves."

(12/14/2004) — The Ho Train were back to their old tricks -- a field goal snafu, special team shenanigans, and an offense that was humbled early and often as they lost to Otters 162-61.

Drew Bennett is definitely the hottest wide out in the league as he scored 3 touchdowns for the second game in a row. Bennett caught 12 passes for a whopping 233 yards. Peyton Manning connected on 26 of 33 pass attempts for 298 yards and 2 touchdowns. He has scored at least twice in each game this season and now has an amazing 46 touchdowns. Torry Holt caught 6 passes for 151 yards including a 75-yard score on a long bomb that he caught and ran in for the score. He has gained over 150-yards and scored in each of the last 2 weeks.

"I think our team responded on the practice field all week," Otters coach Rich Raida added. "We've got a chance to get back to playing good ball. We'll see what happens."

Edgerrin James caught 7 passes for 54 yards and carried 28 times for 104 yards. "Edge" is leading the league in rushing yardage with 1395 yards in 13 games. Jake Delhomme connected on 16 of his 30 pass attempts and gained 206 yards with a 14-yard touchdown strike and 1 interception. He has thrown for a score in 7 straight games. Rookie Lee Evans put on a show in front of family and friends in his home town. He had 1 rushing attempt for 48 yards and 4 catches for 33 yards including a 7-yard touchdown.

"I was happy to get to the stadium. We had a turbulent flight, hotel alarms rang all night, the food stunk, and the bus broke down heading to the stadium, said Jon Kraus, losing coach of the Ho Train. "I would have liked it to have turned out in our favor but that's life."
