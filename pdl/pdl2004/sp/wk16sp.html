(12/28/2004) — Making big plays usually isn't a problem for quarterback Peyton Manning and this game was no different as he helped the Otters to a win over the Da Funk 97-65.

Peyton Manning finally broke the single season touchdown record as he scored twice and threw for over 380 yards. Manning has an incredible 121.4 passer rating. Warrick Dunn earned each of his 52 yards on the ground, but he managed to find the end zone on a 16-yard run. Dunn also added 87 yards receiving, his season high.

"We did a great job answering," Otters coach Rich Raida said. "We took responsibility for our mistakes and turned it around."

Nate Burleson showed his big-play potential with a 68-yard touchdown reception. He finished with 2 catches for 110 yards. Brandon Stokley captured the first 1000-yard season of his career with 7 catches for 123 yards. Stokley capped off his performance with 21-yard touchdown. Rudi Johnson struggled on the ground, averaging just 1.6 yards per carry, but he found the end zone on a 1-yard run. Johnson's 31 yards on the ground was his lowest of the season.

"It was rough tonight," Da Funk coach Mark Stahlsmith said. "I coached about as badly as I can remember coaching in a long time. I've got to improve."
