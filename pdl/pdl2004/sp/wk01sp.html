(9/14/2004)  A strong exhibition season is no guarantee of success. The Carter's Cammandos, unbeaten in the preseason, quickly lost their momentum in the season opener against the Iron City Pounders.

Curtis Martin turned it on in the opener as he accumulated over 200 yards of total offense and scored 2 touchdowns. Jeff Garcia felt good in his new home, as he connected on a 46-yard touchdown pass and added a 3-yard touchdown run.

"You can think about how many fantasy points each of your players have accumulated after the season," said Iron City Pounders coach Brian McIntyre. "But right now the Ws are a lot more important."

Mike Vanderjagt's consecutive field goal streak came to an end this week when he missed a field goal late in the game. Plaxico Burress had an unexciting day, catching just 1 pass for 13 yards. Laveranues Coles didn't have much fun this week as he caught just 3 passes for 27 yards.

"Nobody has surrendered yet," Carter's Cammandos coach Chris Arenth said. "We're trying to keep the pride. It's just getting hard right now."

Pittsburgh (9/14/2004)  After a great mini-camp and a strong preseason, the Scooter's Boozers appeared to have been confused with everything from the shape of the football to the referee's whistle.

No one is surprised when Daunte Culpepper turns in good numbers, but few anticipated seeing a masterpiece in the first game of the season. Culpepper was flawless as he completed 17 of 23 passes for 242 yards and connected on a career high 5 touchdowns. Terrell Owens likes his new scenery a lot. Owens caught 8 passes for 68 yards, but 3 of them happened to be touchdowns. It was Owens first game with 3 touchdowns since early in the 2001 season. LaDainian Tomlinson got off to a better start this season as he rushed for 121 yards and 1 touchdown. Tomlinson also finished the game with negative receiving yardage, something he has never done before.

"At halftime, I was very emotional in the locker room," Manic Moose coach Matt Spinola said. "I care about the team, and I felt I had let people down after my starters got off to a slow start."

Quincy Morgan made the most of his only reception this week. Morgan turned it into a 46-yard touchdown. Keyshawn Johnson had a productive day with 9 catches for 111 yards.

"It's obvious this ballgame flipped in the third quarter," Scooter's Boozers coach Scott Arrigo said. "This was very disappointing coming off two wins and a close game at halftime. Two possessions and a punt return and it's a completely different game."

(9/14/2004)  The offseason was unkind to the Texas Nashvillians. So they took out all of their pent up frustration on the Da Funk in their impressive 100-52 win.

Tom Brady sure didn't repeat last year's disappointing opening day performance. Brady was on target all day long as he threw for 335 yards and 3 touchdowns. Marshall Faulk looked good in the opener as he rushed 22 times for 128 yards. Although Faulk didn't score a touchdown he did run in a 2-pt conversion. Many fantasy owners were pleased to see Eric Moulds healthy again after last season's injury problems. Moulds caught 8 passes for 78 yards, including a 17-yard touchdown.

"We've been through 14 days of being told from every which angle, every which way, in every language, that we're not very good," Texas Nashvillians coach Randy Folks said. "This football team doesn't believe that."

Matt Hasselbeck wasn't spectacular in the opener, but he did manage to throw one touchdown pass and finish with almost 250 yards passing. Rudi Johnson picked up less than 3 yards per carry, but he found the endzone early in the game and finished with 70 yards rushing. Derrick Mason had an extremely quiet day, catching just 3 passes for 51 yards.

"Unbelievable, man," Da Funk coach Mark Stahlsmith said. "That's the way the game bounces."

(9/14/2004)  Sean Berecek is a perfectionist. Even after leading the Blitzkrieg to a 79-53 win the coach refused to accept any compliments, instead moving on to prepare for the next opponent.

Shaun Alexander had a huge day with 3 touchdowns and 166 yards of total offense. Alexander didn't score 3 or more touchdowns in a single game last season. It looked like Chad Pennington was on track for a huge day after throwing 2 touchdowns in the first quarter. But things calmed down and Pennington didn't find the endzone the rest of the game.

"We knew facing a team like Beers that we really would need to step up our play today," said Sean Berecek. "Shaun Alexander did that today."

Emmitt Smith posted decent numbers as he rushed for 87 yards and an 11-yard touchdown. It was the most rushing yards Smith has had in a game in more than a season. Randy Moss didn't get a lot of touches this week, but he made the most of the ones he did. Moss only pulled in 4 catches for 27 yards, but 2 of the catches were for touchdowns. Aaron Brooks was vicitimized by his own teammates as they dropped balls, ran the wrong routes, and allowed constant pressure in the pocket. Brooks finished with 223 yards passing and 1 touchdown.

"In a minute-forty we had a dropped touchdown, two fumbles, a blocked punt, two penalties, and missed tackles for a touchdown," Beers coach Mike Spinola said. "Twenty-eight minutes before that we played good football. I lost my appetite Sunday night."

(9/14/2004)  It was an ugly home opener for the Tally Wackers who spent much of the off season trying to fix two major problems: a defense that allowed too many points and too many yards, and an offense that turned the ball over too frequently.

Peyton Manning was far from perfect, but he still managed to find the endzone on a couple of short touchdown passes. Under pressure all day, Manning only completed 55% of his passes and was picked off once. Warrick Dunn scored a couple of touchdowns and rushed for 63 yards, giving some owners hope that he can be a solid fantasy running back. (9/14/2004)  Quarterbacks seem to get all the credit. This game was no different as Rich Gannon scored 29 points leading the OakalDoakals to a 89-36 victory over Ho Train.

Rich Gannon left most owners wondering what type of season they can expect from him. Although Gannon threw for over 300 yards and 2 long touchdowns he was also responsible for 3 turnovers. Domanick Davis proved to be very versatile this week as he rushed for 87 yards and pulled in 70 yards receiving. Davis also punched in 2 touchdowns from short range. Alge Crumpler turned in a strong performance, catching 6 passes for 82 yards and a 15-yard touchdown. Crumpler had never caught more than 5 passes in a game.

"I think our team responded on the practice field all week," OakalDoakals coach Alan Hatok added. "We've got a chance to get back to playing good ball. We'll see what happens."

Chris Brown didn't post huge fantasy numbers, but he looks ready to have a breakout game. Brown rushed for 100 yards on 16 carries. Michael Vick turned in average numbers (13 of 22, 163 passing yards, 1 TD) while struggling to control the ball. He fumbled twice (neither was lost) and was intercepted once in the game. Jamal Lewis didn't come close to breaking any records this week as he was stymied all day. Lewis rushed for just 57 yards on 20 carries and was held scoreless.

"We kicked ourselves in the teeth the whole game," said Ho Train coach Jon Kraus. "We had the opportunities, we just didn't do anything with them."
